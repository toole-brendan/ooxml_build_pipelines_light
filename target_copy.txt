DECK STYLE SYSTEM — copy, exhibits, color, and type

The deck speaks as an analyst stating conclusions, not a narrator walking
through an analysis. One system governs every visible object: titles, labels,
commentary, captions, callouts, notes, charts, tables, shapes, color, and type.
Lead with the point; use layout, hierarchy, fill, and borders to make the
answer easy to read.


STRUCTURE

Commentary — interprets the exhibit, it does not restate it. Active-voice,
evidence-based readings of what a chart or table means: the trend, the
contrast, the implication behind the numbers — each point tied to a figure or
named specific. The common form is a bold finding sentence followed by
non-bold sentences, usually bulleted, that carry the evidence. The bold lines,
read alone, are the takeaways.

Titles — "Topic | Finding.". Topic is a Title Case noun phrase; Finding carries
the real claim in full — capitalized at the first word after the pipe, sentence
case thereafter — long enough to state the conclusion, short enough to stay
within two lines. - "Finding" also needs to be sentence and ends with "." period.

Section dividers — the subtitle previews the shape of the answer, stated
qualitatively: no hard numbers, no agenda list, no terminal period. State the
answer where there is one; frame the inquiry where there isn't. If a section
has no crisp conclusion yet, drop the subtitle rather than force a claim.


CHART SLIDES

Chart slides pair a measured exhibit with its reading: the chart carries the
quantitative shape, the commentary states the market-sizing implication. A
units caption sits above the chart; the commentary lives outside it.

Chart types — pick by the sizing question:
- Waterfall — builds or bridges a total from signed parts.
- Single-series column — compares levels, rankings, rates, or cadence.
- Clustered column — compares values that must not be summed, including
  nested ones (SAM against TAM).
- Stacked column — mutually exclusive parts of a total, across periods or
  categories.
- 100% column — mix when the absolute total is secondary.
- Marimekko — size and mix together, where tile area represents value.

Layout (for chart slides) — three patterns:
- Pattern A, chart left and commentary right. Chart ~7.7 in by ~3.7 in;
  commentary in one right-side box (Commentary or CommentaryBody), ~4.2 in
  by ~3.4 in, 3–4 finding paragraphs, ~100–140 words. An optional strip
  under the chart carries one evidence, anchor, or implication line.
- Pattern B, full-width chart (~12.5 in) with commentary below as
  cards or table, maybe ~4.0 in by ~1.2 in: a bold mini-title
  and one ~50-word paragraph.
- Pattern C, chart left and commentary right AND below.

Commentary register:
- In terms of styling - usually a no-fill shape with black font text following this pattern -> bolded "parent" sentence + few bulleted, non-bolded supporting sentences (+ newline of empty text / space and then repeat -> bolded "parent" sentence, etc.)


TABLES

Layout
- Table-only slides: full-width table under the title, usually ~12.0–12.5 in
  wide.
- Table plus commentary: follows same layouts as chart pages - Patterns A/B/C.

Copy
- Round consistently in deck notation: $M, $B, %, ~, ranges.
- Order rows and columns by logic: size, priority, chronology, build sequence,
  scenario progression, or implication.
- Separate totals, subtotals, and scenario rows visibly.

Build
- Use horizontal rules only; no vertical borders. Default to a ½ pt gray #5 (646464)
  rule between body rows, a 1 pt rule under the header, and no rule under the last row.
  Step a body-row rule up to 1 pt black where heavier separation reads better.
- Default to an unfilled header carried by its rule; reserve a dark header for
  the primary table on a page.
- Keep heavy black borders to one object family per slide.
- Use ghost rows or columns as invisible gutters: merged where needed,
  near-zero padding, no visible border or fill, usually under ~0.1 in.
- Use color only through the fill rules: usually the "column headers" or "row headers", thin ghost columns (if they're being used - as an "accent" sometimes if "row headers" are used), highlighted rows, or key cells. 
- Size rows to content so evidence grids read even, not ragged. 
- Internal alignment in cells can vary but should be carefully considered for each table individually.
- Also important to keep in mind how font size for table cells compares to row height (will the row look massive compared to the font size?)

Commentary is optional for simple lookup tables and required when the table
carries a sizing implication. 
- In terms of styling - usually a no-fill shape with black font text following this pattern -> bolded "parent" sentence + few bulleted, non-bolded supporting sentences (+ newline of empty text / space and then repeat -> bolded "parent" sentence, etc.)


COLOR AND FILLS

Color is a hierarchy system, not decoration. Use only the house text colors and
the blue/gray ramps; do not introduce one-off colors.

Palette
- Text: BLACK 000000 for default body text; WHITE FFFFFF on dark fills;
  BLACK 000000 for borders and draft chip text.
- Blue ramp: E2E9EF, B6C8D8, 6E91B1, 3D5972, 263746.
- Gray ramp: F2F2F2, D9D9D9, BFBFBF, 7F7F7F, 646464.
- If a slide calls for a placeholder to be used (an image or a chart there is not data for yet) this pale-yellow should be used - FFFFCC - in a rectangle shape with 1 pt black borders - the purpose of the placeholder can be written in the body of the shape / middle-aligned with italicized font. 

Fill logic
- Use fill only when the object has semantic weight: answer/KPI, denominator,
  evidence chip, method badge, unit caveat, program or row identity, focal
  warning.
- Fill across successive shapes can also "ramp" in "intensity" / darkness across palette - or in table's column headers for example
- Keep structural and interpretive text no-fill and usually no-border:
  commentary, legend bullets, method notes, axis labels, captions, row labels,
  divider labels, and explanatory bullets.
- Dark fills use white text. Blue flips to white at the third step; gray flips
  to white at the darkest step.

Borders
- Every shape with any fill color or shade carries a 1 pt black border.
- A no-fill shape usually carries no border (usually bulleted commentary or can be used as caption or chart titles or table titles), but can also have 1 pt 
- Tables follow the same fill logic, but with horizontal rules only as described
  under TABLES.

Connector lines - typically black + can be solid or dashed + should have arrows at one end most of the time - weight is typically ¼-½ pt for flow / leader / divider lines, stepping to 1 pt for emphasis (a non-black line is allowed but should be defined in a legend; caption text is also encouraged on connector lines)
Divider lines can also be used, but not excessively.

TYPOGRAPHY

Use Arial throughout. Type size signals hierarchy; bold carries structure or
value; italic marks qualifiers, captions, subtitles, and connector notes. ALL
CAPS is explicit text, used sparingly for caps and headers.

Core scale
- 8 pt: sources, footnotes, caveats, tiny axis ticks.
- 8.5 pt: fine print, dense sublines, unit notes, chip bodies, qualifiers.
- 9 pt: row, column, bar, segment, and map labels.
- 10 pt: dense body text, ledgers, rails, compact callouts, chart titles.
- 11 pt: message strips, readouts, concise finding sentences.
- 12 pt: default body text; bold/ALL CAPS for card caps, strip caps, and
  in-shape headers.
- 13 pt: exhibit headers, bold and used sparingly.
- 14 pt: compact numeric values inside bars, chips, or small KPI cells.
- 16 pt: badges, row identities, gates, compact anchors.
- 18 pt: ribbon KPIs and high-emphasis strip values.
- 24 pt: answer-card KPI values.
- 32 pt: the one true hero headline on a content slide.

In-shape hierarchy
- Cap + subline: 12 pt bold/ALL CAPS plus 9 pt italic.
- Value + qualifier: 18 pt bold plus 8.5 pt italic.
- Label prefix + value: 8.5 pt bold prefix plus 11 pt value.
- Section + bullets: 10 pt bold label plus 9 pt bullets.
- Map or flow box: 9 pt bold title plus 8.5 pt body.
- Connector note: 8.5 pt italic, no fill, no border.

Chrome sizes (locked by the layout, not part of the scale above)
- Content-slide title "Topic | Finding.": 20 pt.
- Cover and divider title: 28 pt; subtitle: 20 pt italic.
- Preliminary chip 12 pt; sources 8 pt.


TEXT MECHANICS

- In rendered slide text, no em dashes; en dashes only for number ranges.
- Keep natural and domain slashes (Virginia/Columbia, AP/LLTM, GFE/GFP,
  FFATA/FSRS, castings/forgings)
- Avoid "+", "x", and "->" as separators — use "and" or restructure. (UNLESS FOR LIKE A FORMULA OR TO SHOW A TYPE OF "FLOW" - JUST DON'T WANT "SHORTHAND" FOR REAL SENTENCES)
- Keep $XXM, $XXB, ~, %, and program acronyms; no double spaces.
- No meta, hype, or process talk — don't narrate the deck ("this chart shows",
  "as we can see"), describe what a section does, or reach for hype adjectives
  ("robust", "comprehensive", "deep-dive", "leverage"); show the result.
- No self-reference or role labels in body, commentary, or exhibit text (no
  "Summary:", "(internal)", "this exhibit") - the dedicated bottom Note / Source
  footnote line is the exception and does carry those labels.
- No build-process leakage — version tags, phase markers, status notes never
  reach a rendered string.
