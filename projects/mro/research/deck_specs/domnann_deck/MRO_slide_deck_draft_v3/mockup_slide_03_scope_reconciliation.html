How the TAM is bounded The $7.1B sits in the top-left cell: activity that is both (a) ship-MRO in character and (b) contracted out. Adjacent cells explain what's nearby but structurally out of scope - no deduction chain or "total pool" construct needed. The two axes carry the logic.
Top-right: Public-yard labor is budget-side only Four public naval shipyards are NWCF activities; civilian workforce executes depot availabilities without FPDS records. This is where "submarine MRO looks understated" - most nuclear depot work on SSNs, SSBNs, CVNs is in-house.
Bottom-left: PSC 1905 contains hidden MRO Nuclear engineered overhauls are frequently coded as shipbuilding (1905), not J998/J999. An estimated $X B of PSC 1905 would migrate into the MRO TAM under work-type-first classification - the reason the $7.1B is a floor, not a ceiling, on sub MRO.
