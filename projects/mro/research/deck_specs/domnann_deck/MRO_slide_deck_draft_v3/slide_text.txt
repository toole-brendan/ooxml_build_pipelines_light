# TITLE
TAM Sizing Approach

# LEDE
Filtering FPDS contract-award data from 2,539 federal PSCs down to 65 ship-MRO services codes sizes the FY2025 Navy and Coast Guard MRO contracting TAM at $7.1B

# LHS CHART -- APPROACH-FLOW DIAGRAM

Row 1: Filter federal PSCs to USN / USCG services-class codes
	Input: 2,539 PSCs (All federal contract-action codes)
	Filter: USN / USCG services-class codes (J, K, N, H, L, M families)
	Output: ~1,800 Services PSCs

Row 2: Filter to ship MRO codes
	Input: ~1,800 Services PSCs
	Filter: Ship MRO codes (Repair, modification, overhaul, husbanding)

	Input: 65 Ship MRO PSCs

	Output: FY2025 MRO TAM: $7.1B

# BREADCRUMB
Commercial Strategy / MRO Defense Demand Research Overview

# TITLE
Addressable vs Adjacent Spend

# LEDE
Subtracting newbuild (-$38.1B), public-yard labor (-$9.5B), and reactor procurement (-$1.9B) from $56.6B in total FY2025 Navy and Coast Guard ship-related spending leaves the $7.1B MRO contracting TAM

# LHS CHART SUBTITLE
FY2025 Navy and USCG ship-related spending reconciled to MRO contracting TAM ($M)

# LHS CHART -- WATERFALL BARS
Bar A: Total pool - Navy + USCG ship-$ = $56,577M
Bar B: less Newbuild (PSC 1905 all platforms) = -$38,100M
Bar C: less Public yards (OMN 1B4B derivation) = -$9,535M
Bar D: less Reactor procurement (PSC 4470) = -$1,875M
Bar E: MRO TAM (Addressable via contract) = $7,067M

# RHS COMMENTARY (single text box; three bolded parent lines each followed by 2-3 unbolded bullets. The scope-reconciliation TABLE was deleted -- waterfall + x-axis labels on the LHS carry the per-step breakdown.)

**PSC 1905 is not purely newbuild**
	Nuclear-platform depot events (e.g., the $424M HII USS Boise SSN-764 Engineered Overhaul at Newport News) are coded as shipbuilding rather than J998 / J999
	The Services MRO TAM therefore undercounts private-sector sub and carrier depot work bundled under the newbuild PSC

**Public-yard labor is implied, not directly observed**
	The $9.5B figure derives from OMN 1B4B Ship Maintenance total ($11,764M) less Ship Maintenance By Contract CE 928 ($2,228M)
	The four public naval shipyards (Portsmouth, Norfolk, Puget Sound, Pearl Harbor) are NWCF activities; their payroll generates no FPDS record

**Nuclear MRO PSC emptiness explained**
	PSCs J044, K044, and N044 sit at approximately $0 in FY2025
	Reactor maintenance is contracted under ship-level PSC 1905 and PSC 4470 rather than standalone service PSCs

# FOOTER NOTES
(1) Tie-out check: 56,577 less 38,100 less 9,535 less 1,875 equals 7,067
(2) Public-yard labor implied as OMN 1B4B total ($11,764M) minus Ship Maintenance By Contract CE 928 ($2,228M); the four public naval shipyards (Portsmouth, Norfolk, Puget Sound, Pearl Harbor) are NWCF activities and payroll generates no FPDS record
(3) Nuclear MRO PSCs J044, K044, and N044 contain approximately $0 because reactor work is contracted under ship-level PSC 1905 and PSC 4470, not standalone service PSCs

# RHS PROCESS FLOW -- DEPOT ENTRY PIPELINE (MSRA -> MAC-MO -> task order)

MSRA - Master Ship Repair Agreement
NAVSEA certifies that a yard's facilities, workforce, quality system, and safety credentials meet Navy depot-repair standards. MSRA is a prerequisite basic ordering agreement, not a contract to perform work.
Administered by: NAVSEA HQ via SUPSHIP offices
Scale: ~25-30 CONUS yards plus ~10-15 FDNF foreign yards

MAC-MO - Multi-Award Contract, Maintenance and Modernization
RMC awards a region-level multi-award IDIQ pool to MSRA-qualified yards. Awardees then compete individual availability task orders against each other.
Administered by: Regional Maintenance Center (SWRMC / MARMC / SERMC / NW RMC)
Scale: 3-6 awardees per pool, 5-year PoP plus 5-year options

FPTO - Fixed-Price Task Order
RMC contracting officer awards individual availability task orders against third-party planner specifications. Awardees bid against each other on labor hours and material pass-through for specific hull events (CNO avails, DSRAs, DPIAs, EDSRAs).
Administered by: RMC contracting officer
Scale: per-availability, typically $20M-$200M per task order, 4-18 month duration

# FOOTER NOTES
(1) $4.9B in-scope TAM is $5.0B gross J998 and J999 obligations less $85M FMS carve-out
(2) Contractor segments: CONUS full-ship primes (BAE Ship Repair, GD NASSCO, HII / Metro Machine / Marine Hydraulics Intl, Vigor Marine, Continental Maritime, Detyens); Regional yards (East Coast R&F, Colonna's, Alabama Shipyard, Pacific Shipyards Intl, Bay Ship & Yacht, Bayonne Drydock, Tecnico); Technical services, non-shipyard (Epsilon Systems third-party planner, Amentum, Southcoast Welding, MAN Energy Solutions OEM support); Foreign MSRA yards (Sumitomo, Navantia, Hanwha Ocean, UniThai, Cabras Marine, Seatrium, Yokohama Engineering)
(3) IDV scope taxonomy generated via LLM classification of 360 parent IDV descriptions and 471 residual no-IDV awards, applied to each J998 and J999 award

# RHS CALLOUT CARDS -- TAKEAWAYS (header line, body line, blank between cards)

Navy OMN and OPN drive 72% of MRO obligations
Navy sustainment drives the market. The appropriation story is a Navy story, not a DoD-wide one.

OPN concentrates in Command Support Equipment (62%) and Spares (32%)
BA-7 funds installation and modernization electronics, C4ISR, and combat-system integration. BA-8 is spares and consumables. Depot availabilities (DSRAs, DPIAs, CNO avails) are funded through BA-7, not through OMN CE-928 contract maintenance.

RDT&E Defense-Wide contributes $780M, or 11% of MRO obligations
Almost entirely Trident II, SSP, and SMDC sustainment on J-series PSCs. Draper MK7 Trident ($318M FY2025) is the single-largest MRO PIID.

SCN contributes approximately $40M on MRO PSCs
De minimis. Nuclear-platform MRO bundles under PSC 1905 shipbuilding rather than onto MRO PSCs; see Slide 4 for the full scope reconciliation.

# RHS CALLOUT -- FRAME A vs FRAME B NOTE
Frame A (contracting activity) is the industry convention for federal contracting TAM in PE, M&A, and sell-side diligence. Frame B (revenue earned) is closer to a contractor's reported P&L but is not how federal markets are typically sized. This deck stays on Frame A; readers modeling a specific contractor can multiply the relevant segment $ by the apportionment rate above for a first-order Frame B estimate.
