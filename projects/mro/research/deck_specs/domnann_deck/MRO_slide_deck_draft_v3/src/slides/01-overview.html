Briefing Materials / Overview

Overview

Context

  • These materials provide foundational analysis to inform two key decisions: (a) the type of defense work to conduct at the new shipyard, (b) how the Gulf Coast and California candidate sites sit relative to the existing U.S. Navy and U.S. Coast Guard MRO footprint
  • Understanding MRO TAM structure informs Port Alpha's go-to-market sequencing, yard certification investment priorities, and the work-type mix to target on the programming floor
  • Analysis extends prior company research, which previously scoped the Navy and USCG ship market from budget-book authority at the program level, by adding contractor-attributed award-level granularity (PSC x hull x prime contractor) from FPDS
  • Determines FY2025 contract obligations across 65 MRO-class PSCs for the U.S. Navy and U.S. Coast Guard, classified to ~<N> vessel classes and ~<M> prime contractors across six active RMC regions
  • Analysis leverages FPDS contract obligations, Treasury File C appropriations from USAspending, FY2026 President's Budget exhibits, SAM.gov entity registrations, SEC 10-K filings, and federal procurement research publications

Objectives of this document

  • Size and decompose the FY2025 USN and USCG MRO contracting TAM by work segment, vessel category, prime contractor, RMC geography, and funding appropriation
  • Reconcile TAM against adjacent Navy and USCG ship-related spending categories (newbuild, reactor procurement, public-yard labor)
  • Characterize depot-ship-repair entry architecture (MSRA -> MAC-MO -> task order) and prime-contractor landscape at the total-MRO and depot-only levels
  • (Ongoing effort) Extend to the newbuild-side PSC universe (SCN-backed submarine and carrier programs) and forward FY2026 through FY2030 budget-book layering, and characterize the newbuild-side competitive landscape to the same depth as the MRO side
Answers shown are preliminary; fidelity and insights will increase with further analysis, additional data, and expert input.
