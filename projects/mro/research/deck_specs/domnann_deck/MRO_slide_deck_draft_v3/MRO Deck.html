Briefing Materials / Overview

Overview

Context

  • These materials provide foundational analysis to inform two key decisions: (a) the type of defense work to conduct at the new shipyard, (b) how the Gulf Coast and California candidate sites sit relative to the existing U.S. Navy and U.S. Coast Guard MRO footprint
  • Understanding MRO TAM structure informs Port Alpha's go-to-market sequencing, yard certification investment priorities, and the work-type mix to target on the programming floor
  • Analysis extends prior company research, which previously scoped the Navy and USCG ship market from budget-book authority at the program level, by adding contractor-attributed award-level granularity (PSC x hull x prime contractor) from FPDS
  • Determines FY2025 contract obligations across 65 MRO-class PSCs for the U.S. Navy and U.S. Coast Guard, classified to ~<N> vessel classes and ~<M> prime contractors across six active RMC regions
  • Analysis leverages FPDS contract obligations, Treasury File C appropriations from USAspending, FY2026 President's Budget exhibits, SAM.gov entity registrations, SEC 10-K filings, and federal procurement research publications

Objectives of this document

  • Size and decompose the FY2025 USN and USCG MRO contracting TAM by work segment, vessel category, prime contractor, RMC geography, and funding appropriation
  • Reconcile TAM against adjacent Navy and USCG ship-related spending categories (newbuild, reactor procurement, public-yard labor)
  • Characterize depot-ship-repair entry architecture (MSRA -> MAC-MO -> task order) and prime-contractor landscape at the total-MRO and depot-only levels
  • (Ongoing effort) Extend to the newbuild-side PSC universe (SCN-backed submarine and carrier programs) and forward FY2026 through FY2030 budget-book layering, and characterize the newbuild-side competitive landscape to the same depth as the MRO side
Answers shown are preliminary; fidelity and insights will increase with further analysis, additional data, and expert input.

Addressable vs Adjacent Spend|Subtracting newbuild (-$38.1B), public-yard labor (-$9.5B), and reactor procurement (-$1.9B) from $56.6B in total FY2025 Navy and Coast Guard ship-related spending leaves the $7.1B MRO contracting TAM
FY2025 Navy and USCG ship-related spending reconciled to MRO contracting TAM ($M)

PSC 1905 is not purely newbuild

  • Nuclear-platform depot events (e.g., the $424M HII USS Boise SSN-764 Engineered Overhaul at Newport News) are coded as shipbuilding rather than J998 / J999
  • The Services MRO TAM therefore undercounts private-sector sub and carrier depot work bundled under the newbuild PSC

Public-yard labor is implied, not directly observed

  • The $9.5B figure derives from OMN 1B4B Ship Maintenance total ($11,764M) less Ship Maintenance By Contract CE 928 ($2,228M)
  • The four public naval shipyards (Portsmouth, Norfolk, Puget Sound, Pearl Harbor) are NWCF activities; their payroll generates no FPDS record

Nuclear MRO PSC emptiness explained

  • PSCs J044, K044, and N044 sit at approximately $0 in FY2025
  • Reactor maintenance is contracted under ship-level PSC 1905 and PSC 4470 rather than standalone service PSCs
Note: (1) Tie-out check: 56,577 less 38,100 less 9,535 less 1,875 equals 7,067; (2) Public-yard labor implied as OMN 1B4B total ($11,764M) minus Ship Maintenance By Contract CE 928 ($2,228M); the four public naval shipyards (Portsmouth, Norfolk, Puget Sound, Pearl Harbor) are NWCF activities and payroll generates no FPDS record; (3) Nuclear MRO PSCs J044, K044, and N044 contain approximately $0 because reactor work is contracted under ship-level PSC 1905 and PSC 4470, not standalone service PSCs.

Depot entry pipeline - MSRA to MAC-MO to task order
1
MSRA - Master Ship Repair Agreement
NAVSEA certifies that a yard's facilities, workforce, quality system, and safety credentials meet Navy depot-repair standards. MSRA is a prerequisite basic ordering agreement, not a contract to perform work.
Administered by: NAVSEA HQ via SUPSHIP offices Scale: ~25-30 CONUS yards plus ~10-15 FDNF foreign yards
↓
2
MAC-MO - Multi-Award Contract, Maintenance and Modernization
RMC awards a region-level multi-award IDIQ pool to MSRA-qualified yards. Awardees then compete individual availability task orders against each other.
Administered by: Regional Maintenance Center (SWRMC / MARMC / SERMC / NW RMC) Scale: 3-6 awardees per pool, 5-year PoP plus 5-year options
↓
3
FPTO - Fixed-Price Task Order
RMC contracting officer awards individual availability task orders against third-party planner specifications. Awardees bid against each other on labor hours and material pass-through for specific hull events (CNO avails, DSRAs, DPIAs, EDSRAs).
Administered by: RMC contracting officer Scale: per-availability, typically $20M-$200M per task order, 4-18 month duration
Note: (1) $4.9B in-scope TAM is $5.0B gross J998 and J999 obligations less $85M FMS carve-out; (2) Contractor segments: CONUS full-ship primes (BAE Ship Repair, GD NASSCO, HII / Metro Machine / Marine Hydraulics Intl, Vigor Marine, Continental Maritime, Detyens); Regional yards (East Coast R&F, Colonna's, Alabama Shipyard, Pacific Shipyards Intl, Bay Ship & Yacht, Bayonne Drydock, Tecnico); Technical services, non-shipyard (Epsilon Systems third-party planner, Amentum, Southcoast Welding, MAN Energy Solutions OEM support); Foreign MSRA yards (Sumitomo, Navantia, Hanwha Ocean, UniThai, Cabras Marine, Seatrium, Yokohama Engineering); (3) IDV scope taxonomy generated via LLM classification of 360 parent IDV descriptions and 471 residual no-IDV awards, applied to each J998 and J999 award; (4) Trade-specific IDIQs (HM&E, surface coatings, insulation, pipe lagging) account for less than 3% of depot spend.
Source: FPDS FY2025 contract obligations on PSCs J998 and J999 (U.S. Navy and U.S. Coast Guard), classifier-enriched. Data as of April 2026.

Navy OMN and OPN drive 72% of MRO obligations Navy sustainment drives the market. The appropriation story is a Navy story, not a DoD-wide one.
OPN concentrates in Command Support Equipment (62%) and Spares (32%) BA-7 funds installation and modernization electronics, C4ISR, and combat-system integration. BA-8 is spares and consumables. Depot availabilities (DSRAs, DPIAs, CNO avails) are funded through BA-7, not through OMN CE-928 contract maintenance.
RDT&E Defense-Wide contributes $780M, or 11% of MRO obligations Almost entirely Trident II, SSP, and SMDC sustainment on J-series PSCs. Draper MK7 Trident ($318M FY2025) is the single-largest MRO PIID.
