# RHS CALLOUT CARDS (header line, body line, blank between cards)

Why awards-first, not budget-first
Awards tag vendor, PSC, vessel, and period at the transaction level; budget books do not. Budget-first sizing would mix in civilian federal payroll and miss OPN-funded MRO (see Slide 4).

Public-yard activity is a budget-side story
FY25 OMN transferred $7.5B to the 4 public shipyards via RRN customer orders; total activity ~$12-15B with SCN and OPN ship-alt work. Civilian federal workforce executes -- no FPDS record. This is where the "submarine MRO looks understated" story lives.

PSC 1905 embedded MRO -- ~$1.5-2.0B quantified
PSC 1905 is predominantly ship construction but contains material MRO. The $1.5-2.0B range tracks the FPDS footprint of OPN BA-01 L1000 Ship Maintenance ($2.4B FY26 request). Top FY25 items: EB $831M SSN hull treatment, HII $424M Boise EOH. Not double-counted into the $7.1B TAM -- sits adjacent, and is why the TAM is a floor on sub / carrier activity.

# RHS CALLOUT CARDS (header line, body line, blank between cards)

OPN funds ~35% of MRO -- a multi-appropriation market
A procurement appropriation -- OPN -- funds nearly as much MRO as OMN. OMN OP-5 SAG 1B4B shows 44 of 68 FY24 availabilities as OMN-funded, 24 as OPN-funded. A budget-first methodology would have to know which OPN BAs to include; awards tagging sidesteps this.

49% of MRO dollars directly TAS-attributed; 51% imputed
Direct attribution via USAspending /awards/funding/ (Treasury File C). Gaps filled by PSC-bucket peer averages. Rollup totals stable across the split.

Navy OMN + OPN = 72% of MRO
MRO is a Navy story, not DoD-wide. USCG = 4%; other DoD + defense-wide = ~13% combined. But "Navy" here means both OMN (maintenance) and OPN (procurement) -- not just OMN.

OPN concentrates in BA-7 Equipment (62%) and BA-8 Spares (32%)
BA-7 funds equipment installs and C4ISR integration during availabilities -- the hardest bucket for budget-first to find. The separate OPN BA-01 L1000 Ship Maintenance program ($2.4B FY26) codes to PSC 1905, not MRO PSCs (see Slide 3).

RDT&E Defense-Wide = 11%
Almost entirely Trident II, SSP, and SMDC sustainment on J-series PSCs. Draper MK7 Trident ($318M FY2025) is the largest single MRO PIID.

SCN ~$40M on MRO PSCs
De minimis. Nuclear-platform MRO bundles under PSC 1905 shipbuilding, not MRO PSCs -- see Slide 3.

# RHS CALLOUT -- FRAME A vs FRAME B NOTE

Frame A = the flow of contract dollars the market will transact -- the right number for market-entry, capability-build, and acquisition decisions. Frame B approximates existing-contractor revenue -- the right number for modeling specific public comps. This deck stays on Frame A. For single-contractor modeling, multiply the relevant segment $ by that segment's apportionment rate (above), not the blended 47.5% -- the 19-71% spread makes the blended rate unsafe for single-segment use.

# FOOTER NOTES
(1) FY2025 window: 2024-10-01 to 2025-09-30. POP apportionment uses start_date and end_date on FPDS award rows (100% populated).
(2) POP is the contract window, not the delivery window. Short-POP segments (depot availabilities) deliver in weeks / months; Frame B linear undercounts. Long-POP segments (combat-systems engineering) can span 5+ years; linear is reasonable. Segment-level calibration pending.
(3) M1 Pure Linear is primary; M2-M4 bound the range.
