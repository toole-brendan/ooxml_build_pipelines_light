# LHS CHART -- FUNNEL
Box 1: 2,539 Active PSCs -- Every federal contract-action code category
	Arrow label: USN / USCG only, Services codes only

	Arrow (no label)

# RHS TABLE -- NOTE / DETAIL
Row 1: PSC | A 4-character Product and Service Code assigned by the contracting officer at the buying command to every federal contract action.
Row 2: Awards data vs. budget materials | FPDS obligations capture executed contract-level spend, attributing dollars to vendor, work type, and hull program. Navy and Coast Guard budget exhibits report planned maintenance at the program level, with no contractor attribution and no PSC-level work-type detail.
Row 3: In scope | All U.S. Navy and U.S. Coast Guard vessel types. Work types: depot ship repair, equipment maintenance, modification, installation, QC and inspection, OEM technical representation, and husbanding services.
Row 4: Out of scope | Civilian federal workforce at the four public naval shipyards (Portsmouth, Norfolk, Puget Sound, Pearl Harbor) -- NWCF-funded, generates no FPDS record; RCOH and reactor-plant sustainment (bundled under shipbuilding PSCs); newbuild and product procurement

# LEGEND

# RHS CALLOUT CARDS -- TAKEAWAYS (parent bullets + sub-bullets)

[FOREGROUND] 49% of MRO $ directly TAS-attributed; 51% imputed from peer PSCs
	Direct attribution via USAspending /awards/funding/ endpoint (Treasury File C)
	Unmatched awards inherit appropriation mix of directly-attributed peers in same PSC bucket
	Rollups stable across the split; per-award precision lower on the imputed subset

Navy OMN + OPN drive 72% of MRO obligations
	Dominant funding source -- this is a Navy story, not DoD-wide
	USCG is only 4%

Within MRO PSCs, OPN concentrates in BA-7 (62%) and BA-8 (32%)
	BA-7 Command Support Equipment: installation / modernization electronics, C4ISR, combat-system integration during availabilities
	BA-8 Spares & Repair Parts
	Availabilities co-funded: OMN 65% / OPN 35% by count (OMN OP-5 1B4B, FY24)
	OPN Ship Maintenance itself is BA-01 Line 1000 ($2.4B FY26, PL 116-93) -- likely flows through PSC 1905, not MRO PSCs

RDT&E Defense-Wide contributes 11%
	Almost entirely Trident II / SSP / SMDC sustainment on J-series PSCs
	Draper MK7 Trident ($318M FY25) is the largest single MRO PIID

SCN contributes ~$40M on MRO PSCs
	De minimis; nuclear-platform MRO bundles under PSC 1905 shipbuilding (see Slide 3)

# FOOTER NOTES
(1) Direct TAS attribution via USAspending /awards/funding/ endpoint; imputed attribution applies per-PSC-bucket appropriation ratios from the directly-classified peer sample to awards without a matched TAS. Rollup totals are stable across the direct / imputed split
(2) OPN BA-01 Line Item 1000 Ship Maintenance, Repair and Modernization per OPN Vol 1 Exhibit P-40 BA-01 BSA-10; OMN / OPN availability co-funding per OMN Vol 1 OP-5 SAG 1B4B Pages 1-3; method in docs/methodology/METHODOLOGY_AVAILABILITY_FUNDING_APPROPRIATIONS.md
(3) All figures on a post-exclusion ($7,067M) basis. Exclusions ($1.7B across seven categories: cross-platform engineering IDIQs, aviation MRO, shore-base facilities, non-Navy, LLM-flagged, FMS, inactive vessels) are treated proportionally across appropriations; see Slide 3 footnote (3) for category breakdown

# BREADCRUMB
Appendix / TAM Framing

# TITLE
TAM Framing

# LEDE
$7.1B Frame A obligations apportion to a $3.3-3.8B range of Frame B (POP-apportioned) FY25-earned contractor revenue

# TOP STRIP -- Obligations x Apportionment = Revenue

Obligations
	$7,067M Frame A (FPDS FY2025 MRO TAM, this deck's primary number)

Apportionment
	Share of each award's period-of-performance landing inside FY2025

Revenue
	$3.27B - $3.75B Frame B range (POP-apportioned contractor revenue)

# LHS CHART SUBTITLE
FY2025 Services MRO TAM by period-of-performance apportionment method ($M)

# LHS CHART -- LEGEND
Frame A -- contract obligations (deck primary)
Frame B -- revenue earned approximation, POP-apportioned (sensitivity)
Central Frame B estimate ~$3.5B (49% of Frame A)

# RHS CALLOUT -- WHICH FRAME TO USE (parent bullet + sub-bullets)

Frame A vs Frame B -- choose by decision
	Frame A (obligations, $7.1B): market-entry, capability-build, acquisition decisions
	Frame B (earned revenue, $3.3-3.8B): modeling specific public comps on earned-revenue basis
	Deck uses Frame A
	For Frame B, multiply each segment's $ by its apportionment rate -- the blended 47.5% is unsafe (19-71% spread across segments)

# FOOTER NOTES
(1) FY2025 window: 2024-10-01 to 2025-09-30. POP apportionment uses start_date and end_date fields on FPDS award rows (100% populated in the MRO dataset)
(2) POP is the contract window, not the work-delivery window. For short-POP segments (depot availabilities deliver in weeks to months) Frame B linear undercounts actual FY25 delivery; for long-POP segments (combat-systems engineering services can span 5+ years) Frame B linear is reasonable
(3) M1 Pure Linear is the primary Frame B estimate; M2 through M4 are bounds on reasonable apportionment assumptions
