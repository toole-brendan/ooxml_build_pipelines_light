# Services / MRO Deck - Actual State

Documents the current 4-slide Services / MRO deck as delivered. Transcribed
from slide screenshots captured 2026-04-18 (stored alongside this file:
`Screenshot 2026-04-18 at 1.49.31 PM.png` through `1.50.26 PM.png`).

All figures are FY2025 U.S. Navy + U.S. Coast Guard Ship MRO TAM, sourced
from FPDS contract obligations across 68 services PSCs, post-exclusions.
Data as of April 2026.

Style: management-consulting due-diligence format. Navy-blue / slate-gray /
light-blue palette. Every slide uses an action-statement title with a
`Topic | assertion` pattern, a subtitle describing the visual, a central
chart on the left, a supporting table or callouts on the right, and a data
source footer.

## Slide 1 - TAM & Scope

**Title:** TAM & Scope | FY2025 Navy and Coast Guard MRO TAM totaled $7.1B

**Left visual - filter funnel** (four stacked boxes, top to bottom, darkening
from light-gray to dark navy):

1. **2,539 Active PSCs** - *Every federal contract-action code category*
2. *(arrow labeled: USN / USCG only, Services codes only)*
3. **~1,800 Services-class PSCs** - *Services-only codes (J, K, N, H, L, M
   families); products excluded*
4. *(arrow labeled: MRO codes only)*
5. **68 Ship MRO PSCs** - *Navy + USCG ship repair, modification, overhaul
   and husbanding codes*
6. *(arrow)*
7. **FY2025 MRO TAM = $7.1B** (dark navy highlight box)

**Right visual - Note / Detail table** (four rows):

| Note | Detail |
|---|---|
| PSC | A 4-character Product and Service Code assigned by the contracting officer at the buying command to every federal contract action. |
| Awards data vs. budget materials | FPDS obligations capture executed contract-level spend, attributing dollars to vendor, work type, and hull program. Navy and Coast Guard budget exhibits report planned maintenance at the program level, with no contractor attribution and no PSC-level work-type detail. |
| In scope | All U.S. Navy and U.S. Coast Guard vessel types. Work types: depot ship repair, equipment maintenance, modification, installation, QC and inspection, OEM technical representation, and husbanding services. |
| Out of scope | In-house labor at the four public naval shipyards (Portsmouth, Norfolk, Puget Sound, and Pearl Harbor); RCOH and reactor-plant sustainment (bundled under shipbuilding PSCs); newbuild and product procurement. |

**Footer:** Source: FPDS FY2025 contract obligations (U.S. Navy + U.S. Coast
Guard, 68 services PSCs, post-exclusions). Data as of April 2026.

## Slide 2 - Vessel Mix

**Title:** Vessel Mix | Surface combatants, amphibious warfare ships, and
submarines accounted for ~62% of FY2025 MRO TAM

**Subtitle:** FY2025 MRO TAM by Vessel Type x Work Segment ($M)

**Left visual - 100% stacked Marimekko-style bar chart**, 6 columns (vessel
categories), y-axis 0-100%, $M totals above each bar. Within-bar segments
labeled with % when the slice is large enough. Columns:

| Column | Total | Depot Ship Repair | HM&E | Combat Systems | Electronics & C4ISR | Port & Technical |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Surface Combatants | $2,120M | 85% | 6% | 6% | 4% | - |
| Amphibious Warfare Ships | $1,440M | 93% | 5% | - | 1% | - |
| Submarines | $838M | 53% | 19% | 22% | 5% | 1% |
| Combat Logistics Ships | $770M | 83% | 11% | - | - | 5% |
| Aircraft Carriers | $422M | 86% | 8% | - | 5% | 1% |
| Other | $1,474M | 40% | 33% | 1% | 14% | 11% |

Legend (under chart): Depot Ship Repair | Hull, Mechanical & Electrical
(HM&E) | Port & Technical Services | Combat Systems Sustainment |
Electronics & C4ISR Sustainment.

**Right margin - two callouts:**

- **Concentration tracks fleet size and maintenance tempo:** DDG-51
  destroyers, LPD/LHD amphibious warships, and Virginia-class SSNs made up
  the bulk of FY2025 MRO spend.
- **Submarine spend is structurally understated:** An estimated ~$4-6B of
  annual nuclear depot work is performed in-house at the four public naval
  shipyards (Portsmouth, Norfolk, Puget Sound, and Pearl Harbor) and does
  not generate FPDS contract records. Only private-sector OEM and specialty
  work is captured here.

**Footer:** Source: FPDS FY2025 contract obligations (U.S. Navy + U.S. Coast
Guard, 68 services PSCs, post-exclusions). Data as of April 2026.

## Slide 3 - MRO Work Segments

**Title:** MRO Work Segments | Depot ship repair made up ~68% of FY2025 MRO
TAM, while adjacent sustainment segments were smaller and more specialized

**Subtitle:** FY2025 MRO TAM by Work Segment ($M)

**Left visual - single 100% vertical stacked bar** (Total FY2025 MRO TAM =
$7,067M), segments bottom to top, darker-to-lighter blue:

| Work Segment | % of TAM |
|---|---:|
| Depot Ship Repair | 68% |
| Hull, Mechanical & Electrical (HM&E) | 13% |
| Combat Systems Sustainment | 8% |
| Electronics & C4ISR Sustainment | 6% |
| Port & Technical Services | 5% |

Inline legend at top-right of chart panel.

**Right visual - Work Segment / Coverage table:**

| Work Segment | Coverage |
|---|---|
| Depot Ship Repair | Whole-ship maintenance availabilities at Pacific and Atlantic Regional Maintenance Centers, awarded through MAC-MO IDIQ task orders. |
| Hull, Mechanical & Electrical (HM&E) | Propulsion accessories, pumps, valves, piping, HVAC, diesel engines, and ship structural systems. |
| Combat Systems Sustainment | Weapons, fire control, VLS, guided missiles (including Trident II sustainment on Ohio-class SSBNs), and aircraft launch/arresting equipment. |
| Electronics & C4ISR Sustainment | Afloat C4ISR - radar, sonar, radio and network systems, navigation, alarms, and electrical signal equipment. |
| Port & Technical Services | Quality control, testing and inspection; OEM technical representation; husbanding (fuel, transport, port visits); and shipyard operations support. |

**Callout box (light-blue, italic, below the table):** Note: ~84% of the
$4.8B depot segment is awarded through a single three-tier structure -
MSRA pre-qualification -> MAC-MO IDIQ -> fixed-price task orders against
third-party planner specs. Entry requires qualification at all three levels.

**Footnotes:**
- (1) Nuclear Propulsion Sustainment PSCs (J044/K044/N044) appear at ~$0
  because reactor work is contracted under ship-level shipbuilding codes at
  HII Newport News, Fluor Marine, and Bechtel - not standalone services PSCs.
- Source: FPDS FY2025 contract obligations (U.S. Navy + U.S. Coast Guard,
  68 services PSCs, post-exclusions). Data as of April 2026.

## Consistent elements across all four slides

- **Title format:** `Topic | action-statement sentence` at top, single line
  where possible.
- **Layout:** left-side chart (~55-60% of slide width), right-side table or
  callout column (~40-45%), footer source strip at the bottom.
- **Color palette:** four-step navy / slate / light-blue sequence for
  stacked-bar segments; dark navy reserved for the hero number (Slide 1)
  and the #1 emphasis header (Slide 4). Medium-gray for neutral Top-10
  bars (Slide 4).
- **Call-out boxes:** light-blue fill, italic text, used on Slides 3 and 4
  for the highest-leverage commercial insight on each page.
- **Footer source strip:** always the same FPDS FY2025 + post-exclusions
  + "Data as of April 2026" formulation (Slide 4 adds SEC 10-K filings).
