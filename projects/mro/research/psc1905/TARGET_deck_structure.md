# Target MRO Deck Structure

_Target architecture for the next iteration of the MRO deck. 23 visible slides, restructured around a TAM → SAM → Competitive Dynamics narrative spine. Derived from the current v1.18 state (23 slides) with one slide cut, one slide added, several reorders, and section / slide renames._

---

## Narrative arc

The deck walks a single funnel end-to-end:

1. **TAM Sizing** — establish the conceptual ladder, derive the MRO TAM, and decompose it by vessel and work segment.
2. **SAM Narrowing & Entry** — narrow to Marauder-like fleet, intersect with depot ship repair to size SAM, then show how SAM is captured (spend structure, geography, contract vehicles).
3. **Competitive Dynamics** — show who captures TAM and depot-segment dollars today, plus the cleanest public services comp.
4. **Appendix** — reconciliation and appropriation detail.

## Summary of changes vs. current deck

- **Reorder**: Definitions moved before the TAM derivation funnel. Vessel Taxonomy and Fleet Reference moved before TAM Composition so the 6-bucket vessel vocabulary is established before any slide applies it.
- **Section renames**: Reframed to track the TAM → SAM arc (Commercial Strategy → TAM Sizing; Market Segmentation → SAM Narrowing & Entry; Competitive Dynamics unchanged; Appendix unchanged).
- **Slide rename**: "Marauder-Like Platform TAM" → "Marauder-Like Fleet MRO" to stop implying a second TAM rung on the ladder — the slide is a filtered view, not a redefined TAM.
- **New slide**: "SAM Sizing" added as the missing funnel step intersecting Marauder-like fleet with depot ship repair.
- **Re-scope**: "Depot Ship Repair Structure" → "Depot Spend Structure"; MSRA / MAC-MO / FPTO architecture prose removed (that story now lives only on Contract Vehicles & Qualifications).
- **Compression**: Vessel Taxonomy shrunk to focus on the SECNAV-to-6-bucket mapping; exhaustive hull class enumeration and placeholder percentage row removed.
- **Cut**: TAM Framing (Frame A vs. Frame B period-of-performance apportionment) — answers a revenue-recognition question boss has disclaimed, and carries a flagged data anomaly.
- **Moved to Appendix (still visible)**: Appropriation Sourcing — off-theme for the main walk but kept visible as backup.

## Cut from target

**TAM Framing** — *current S23, removed entirely*
The existing slide converts FY25 contract obligations into FY25-delivered contractor revenue by apportioning awards across their period of performance — a revenue-recognition exercise. Boss explicitly said he does not care about revenue recognition. The slide also carries a flagged data anomaly (two of the four apportionment columns return values roughly 15× the headline range, because the workbook sums the full multi-year stream rather than the FY25-delivered portion). Cut rather than hidden because no other slide depends on the Frame A / Frame B framing and the slide would need material rework before it could ship even if kept.
