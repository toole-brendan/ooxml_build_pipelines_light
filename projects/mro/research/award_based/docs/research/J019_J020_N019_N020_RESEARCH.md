# J019 / J020 / N019 / N020 - Navy + Coast Guard Ship Equipment M&R and Installation: Research Brief

**Scope.** FY2025 USAspending awards filtered to PSCs J019, J020, N019, N020. Totals: **1,462 awards / 2,941 mods / $0.98B FY25 obligation**. Source: `data_pull/output/fpds/j019_j020_n019_n020_awards.json`.

## 1. What these four PSCs actually mean

Two PSC letter families, two sub-categories each:

| PSC | Name | What it buys |
|---|---|---|
| **J019** | Maint/Repair/Rebuild of Equipment - Ships, Small Craft, Pontoons, Floating Docks | M&R of whole vessels and vessel-sized platforms - but the *small, non-commissioned* kind (boats, craft, floating drydocks, barges, pontoons, causeways) OR specialized vessel-scale services (preservation, underwater hull husbandry, post-shakedown deficiency work) |
| **J020** | Maint/Repair/Rebuild of Equipment - Ship and Marine Equipment | M&R of *components* that are installed on ships - diesel engines, pumps, motors, davits, sonar arrays, photonics masts, radars, electronic warfare suites, SSTGs |
| **N019** | Installation of Equipment - Ships, Small Craft, Pontoons, Floating Docks | Installation work performed *on* ships and small craft (physical install / integration / modernization work onto the hull, performed by Alteration Installation Teams) |
| **N020** | Installation of Equipment - Ship and Marine Equipment | Installation of marine-equipment systems - often the hybrid case where the *equipment* is the primary deliverable but there is a physical install component (CG C5I integration, NAVAIR shipboard aviation systems, autonomous/unmanned maritime systems) |

The critical distinction vs J998/J999 (whole-ship CNO availabilities, $5.01B): these PSCs are **not** full-ship availability work. They buy either (a) component-level M&R of individual ship equipment, or (b) installation of equipment onto ships. The J998/J999 work happens *during* a scheduled availability; a lot of J019/J020/N019/N020 work happens *between* availabilities, or on ships and craft that never enter a CNO availability cycle (Coast Guard cutters, small boats, MSC ships).

**Aggregate numbers:**

| PSC | Awards | FY25 $ | Median award | Character |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| J019 | 415 | $488M | $93K | Small-craft / SLEP / UHH / PSA / preservation |
| J020 | 937 | $300M | $29K | Component-level equipment M&R, high-frequency low-$ |
| N019 | 75 | $85M | $435K | Almost entirely NIWC Atlantic C4ISR AIT installs |
| N020 | 35 | $108M | $774K | CG C5I integration + NAVAIR shipboard systems + autonomy |

The J999/J998 comparison: J998/J999 averaged $1.75M per award across 2,861 awards. These four together average $672K across 1,462 awards and are a mix of high-volume low-$ (J020) and small-count high-$ (N020). Different character of work, different buyers, different sellers.

## 2. J019 - Small craft, floating docks, and specialized vessel services ($488M)

Contrary to the PSC title, J019 is *not* mostly generic "ship M&R." The FY25 data sorts into distinct programs:

**(a) Coast Guard 47 MLB Service Life Extension Program - $49M to Birdon America.** Parent IDV `70Z02319DAML00300` explicitly titled "47 MLB SLEP EXTENDS THE SERVICE LIFE OF 107 (MIN) TO 117 (MAX) MLBs." Birdon America (Colorado HQ, Bellingham WA + Portland CT facilities) holds the entire CG Motor Lifeboat rebuild program. 10-year contract awarded 2019, extended 2024. Background: CG is rebuilding 47-foot motor lifeboats (their primary surf rescue platform, surf-to-20-ft capable, self-righting) to extend fleet through 2047 under a ~$191M program. FY25 captures two task orders ($34M + $13M) plus smaller mods.

**(b) Underwater Hull Husbandry - $38M to Seaward Marine Services.** Parent IDV N0002423D4311 "SERVICES PERIOD." Two large task orders titled "PACIFIC FLEET" ($19M) and "ATLANTIC FLEET" ($16M) - standing fleet-wide UHH contracts for underwater hull cleaning, inspection, and limited underwater repair. UHH is a specialized diving / ROV operation performed pierside; distinct from drydock work because the ship doesn't leave the water.

**(c) Submarine Preservation - $32M IDV** (`N4215821DS002` "SUBMARINE PRESERVATION MAINTENANCE SVCS"), ~$14M captured in FY25 to International Marine and Industrial Applicators (IMIA). Specialized industrial coating work on SSNs/SSBNs. Related: Bremerton Inactive Shipyard labor work via OWL International ($30M across two contracts) - preservation of decommissioned submarines awaiting dismantlement.

**(d) Post-Shakedown Availabilities (PSA) - $39M.** A PSA is an industrial-activity availability scheduled 6-11 months after a newly-built ship's delivery to correct deficiencies found during shakedown cruises. PSC is J019 because it is technically M&R work on the whole ship before it enters the normal CNO cycle.
- DDG-128 (USS Ted Stevens) PSA - $18.7M to HII
- LCS Post-Shakedown Availability planning IDV `N0002419G2318` - $20.6M to Austal USA (third-party planning + execution for LCS PSAs)

**(e) Navy Expeditionary Combat Craft (NECC) IDIQs - ~$34M across four IDVs** (`N5523621D0013/14/15`, `N5523620D0004`). These are Code 410B multiple-award contracts for NECC repairs and industrial support - the Navy's expeditionary craft inventory (riverine command boats, coastal patrol craft, MK VI patrol boats).

**(f) Barge/Boat and Watercraft IDIQs.** `N4215822DS003 BARGE/BOAT IDIQ OVERHAUL` ($7M), `N0016720D0007 WATERCRAFT INDUSTRIAL SUPPORT TO INCLUDE CONSTRUCTION, PROOF OF DESIGN, MODEL DESIGN` ($8M).

**(g) Floating dry dock work - $13M** for USS BANGOR CAISSON maintenance (Trident refit facility) via Everett Ship Repair.

Top contracting offices show this mix: NAVSEA HQ $138M (PSA BOAs + large programs), SWRMC San Diego $64M (UHH Pacific + craft IDIQs), Norfolk Naval Shipyard GF $43M, SUPSHIP Conversion and Repair $42M (new construction warranty work), Puget Sound IMF $31M (inactive sub preservation). The Coast Guard sliver is $49M via HQ Contract Operations (CG-912) + SFLC Procurement Branch 3 ($6M of small routine work) - almost entirely the MLB SLEP program.

Geography mirrors this: VA $140M, CA $96M, **CO $49M** (that's Birdon America HQ), WA $43M, MS $30M (HII Pascagoula), AL $21M (Austal Mobile for LCS PSA). The CO concentration is the single clearest signal that a non-shipyard prime runs a major ship program.

## 3. J020 - Component-level ship equipment M&R ($300M)

This is the most fragmented and technically-diverse of the four PSCs. 937 awards with median $29K means high-volume transactional buying. The top task orders tell the story of what "ship and marine equipment" means in practice:

**(a) Towed sonar arrays and acoustic sensors.** Leidos $20M at NAVSEA Crane for "Naval Array Technical Support Center to manufacture, upgrade, repair, and refurbish towed array modules and component parts for all U.S. Navy" platforms. This is USW sonar work.

**(b) Electronic warfare systems.** TriStar Inc. $21M for Maritime Electromagnetic Warfare Systems Division (MEWSD) at NSWC Crane. Crane is the Navy's EW center of excellence - AN/SLQ, AN/SRS-family gear.

**(c) Submarine SSTGs.** Northrop Grumman $8.6M for "791 Delaware and 782 Mississippi SSTG No. 1 and No. 2 Material" - Ship Service Turbine Generator work on two Virginia-class boats. SSTGs provide electrical power from steam off the reactor.

**(d) Submarine photonics (periscope successor).** L3 Technologies $8.7M via IDV `N6660424DR400` PHOTONICS DEPOT SERVICES. Virginia-class boats use photonics masts (digital optical imagery + ESM) instead of traditional periscopes; this is depot-level M&R of the photonic mast assemblies.

**(e) Diesel engines for MSC + small craft.** $19M via IDV `N3220521D4105` "DIESEL ENGINE IDIQ" to MAN Energy Solutions. MSC's program-manager contract for diesel engine overhauls across the auxiliary fleet. Related: `N3220525D4047` Engineering / HVAC / Refrigeration Supplies and Services for MSC Fleet.

**(f) MCM USV in-service engineering.** Textron $14M for Mine Countermeasures Unmanned Surface Vessel engineering support - the Navy's new MCM USV feeds LCS + future MCM mission packages.

**(g) BAE Tech Solutions Lifetime Support Engineering (LSE).** $16M - component-level lifetime sustainment contract.

**(h) SEAPORT-NXG task orders.** Multiple parent IDVs (`N0017819D8006/D8739/D8729/D7693`) totaling ~$55M. SEAPORT-NXG is NAVSEA's catch-all professional engineering support IDIQ; when a narrow component-level task needs expertise (obsolescence management, cable refurbishment, radar overhaul), it typically gets issued as a SEAPORT-NXG task order. This is the "all-purpose services" vehicle for anything J020-flavored.

**(i) Coast Guard tail - $27M across 538 small awards.** SFLC (Surface Forces Logistics Center) Procurement Branches 1, 2, and 3 account for 538 of the 937 J020 awards but only $27M - micropurchases for cutter component M&R (pump rebuilds, valve overhauls, motor replacements, propeller shaft work).

NAICS mix confirms the component-fragmentation: 336611 (Ship Building) only $91M, **541330 (Engineering Services) $89M**, 334511 (Navigation/Detection sensors) $39M, 333618 (Diesel Engines) $25M, 811310 (Commercial Machinery Repair) $20M. J020 is an engineering-services bucket more than a shipyard bucket.

Geography: VA $67M, MD $43M (Pax River / Annapolis), IN $27M (Crane), CA $26M, RI $22M (Newport / NUWC), HI $16M, FL $13M. The IN dollar figure for a "ship" PSC is distinctive - that's NSWC Crane entirely.

Keyword frequency inside the mod text confirms the component character: diesel engine 45 awards / $25M; davit/crane 33/$16M; motor/generator 22/$10M; photonics/masts 14/$6M; hull cleaning 23/$2M; pumps 15/$600K; propeller/shaft 23/$2M.

## 4. N019 - Afloat C4ISR Installation ($85M)

N019 is a single-purpose bucket: **$69M of $85M flows through NIWC Atlantic**, and the top five parent IDVs are all the same program - "AFLOAT GLOBAL C4ISR INSTALLATION SERVICES" (IDVs `N0003919D0020/D0022/D0023/D0024/D0025`). This is the NAVWAR / NIWC Atlantic multiple-award IDIQ for afloat C4ISR installations, originally awarded August 2019 with a $2.45B 10-year ceiling to six primes.

Six-company winner set is visible in the J+N019 data and elsewhere in this extract:
- **M.C. Dean $31M** - shipboard electrical integrator
- **VT MILCOM $22M** (VT Group) - press-released August 2019 as one of the six winners
- **PRISM Maritime $15M** - also wins N020 work
- **Serco Inc. $12M**
- **HII Fleet Support Group $3M**

Hull-level specificity is unusually clean for installation work - **54% of N019 $ is tagged to a specific hull**, because NIWC Atlantic issues task orders by hull (`TOID 4991: USS HAMPTON (SSN-767)`, `TOID 5110: USS LABOON (DDG 58)`, etc.). Visible hulls: DDG-55 Stout, DDG-58 Laboon, DDG-64 Carney, DDG-74 McFaul, DDG-78 Porter, LPD-21 New York, LSD-51 Oak Hill, SSN-767 Hampton, CVNs, LHDs.

Work-type context from mod text and IDV descriptions:
- C4ISR alterations (radios, networks, crypto, sensors)
- Air and Missile Defense capability installs (PRISM's specialization)
- Virginia-class submarine Common Submarine Radio Room (CSRR) installs

What N019 actually captures: the *labor* and *installation services* for putting government-furnished C4ISR kits onto ships during modernization periods. The kit itself is typically GFE; the AIT physically mounts the racks, runs cables, integrates with existing combat systems, and executes System Integration Testing (SIT) and Operational Testing (OT). Performance is almost entirely in VA ($69M / 81%) with small tails in FL (Mayport), CT (Groton), GA (Kings Bay).

## 5. N020 - Installation of Ship and Marine Equipment ($108M)

N020 is the smallest of the four buckets by award count (35 awards), but it averages the highest $/award of any of them ($774K median). Work mix is structurally different from N019 - not a single IDIQ program, but four distinct program streams:

**(a) NAWCAD Patuxent River - $40M to BAE Systems Technology Solutions & Services.** Parent IDVs `N0042125D0029/D0016/D0047` (LIFETIME SUPPORT ENGINEERING, LABOR-CPFF). Despite "NAVAIR" association, NAWCAD handles shipboard-installed aviation / aviation-support systems: catapult + arresting gear, air traffic control, aviation fuel handling, carrier-based weapons systems. The N020 coding reflects the installation component, not just equipment.

**(b) NAVFAC Systems and Expeditionary Warfare Center - $29M to Oceanetics Inc.** Parent IDV `N3943023D4050` "OI SUPPORT SERVICES (CPFF 60-MONTHS)." Work includes "HYDROPHIS installation test," "Advanced Interface Assembly (AIA)," "Active Handling System production" - autonomous maritime systems / UUV interface hardware. This is NAVFAC EXWC's (Port Hueneme) expeditionary / unmanned work.

**(c) USCG C5I Shipboard Integration - $27M to Vector Planning & Services (VPSI) via NIWC Atlantic.** Parent `N0017819D8789` SEAPORT-NXG task order explicitly titled "UNITED STATES COAST GUARD (USCG) SHIPBOARD COMMAND, CONTROL, COMMUNICATIONS, COMPUTERS, CYBER, INTELLIGENCE (C5I) SYSTEMS ENGINEERING, INTEGRATION." Interesting cross-service pattern: Navy NIWC Atlantic contract office supporting CG shipboard C5I work, likely because NIWC Atlantic has the AIT infrastructure the CG lacks.

**(d) PRISM Maritime Air & Missile Defense installs - $10M** (parent `N0016422CJQ86`).

Geography: MD $41M (Pax River), CA $40M (San Diego / NAVAIR PMA-260 / EXWC), FL $16M, VA $11M. NAICS is 90% 541330 engineering services - this is integration engineering with a hardware installation component, not a shipyard labor bucket.

## 7. Hull mix - mostly unclassified, and why

Specific hull tagging is low for these PSCs (15-30%). This is structural, not a classification gap:

- **J019 $402M of $488M (82%) is unclassified** because the work is boat/craft/pontoon-level (not tied to a hull class), or a fleet-wide services contract (UHH Pacific Fleet, Atlantic Fleet), or a program vehicle covering many platforms (MLB SLEP).
- **J020 $258M of $300M (86%) is unclassified** because components are shared across many hull classes (a diesel engine overhaul serves any ship with that engine).
- **N019 $47M of $85M (55%) is unclassified** because C4ISR programs span the fleet; but because NIWC Atlantic writes task orders by hull, 45% *is* hull-tagged (much higher than J020).
- **N020 $106M of $108M (99%) is unclassified** because the work is installation-engineering-services, not hull-specific execution.

Visible hull-tagged work is dominated by DDGs ($48M across all four PSCs), submarines (SSN/SSBN $33M - photonics, SSTGs, C4ISR CSRR installs, preservation), LCS ($25M - PSA + MCM USV-related), T-AO ($20M - MSC diesel engine overhauls).

## 8. How this relates to the J998/J999 model

J998/J999 = $5.01B of whole-ship availability work (DSRA/SRA/CMAV/DPIA etc.)

These four PSCs = $0.98B of *adjacent* work, split roughly:
- **$488M J019** - non-availability-cycle M&R (boats / craft / preservation / PSA / UHH)
- **$300M J020** - component-level M&R by NSWCs and specialized IDIQs (diesel, EW, sonar, photonics)
- **$85M N019** - C4ISR modernization installs performed on commissioned ships
- **$108M N020** - cross-command engineering-integration work with an install component

Roughly 20% of J998/J999 spending, but structurally different: the buyers are different (NSWCs and NAVWAR, not RMCs), the primes are different (engineering-services firms and component OEMs, not shipyards), and the work occurs outside the big availability windows.

One architectural read: J998/J999 is the *ship* market (big-dollar contiguous work on an individual hull). These four PSCs together are the *equipment* and *modernization* market - fragmented across NSWC Crane (Indiana), NSWC Newport (Rhode Island), NSWC Philadelphia, NAVSEA HQ, NIWC Atlantic (Charleston), NAWCAD Pax River, NAVFAC EXWC Port Hueneme. Each center procures its own specialization.

### Cross-cuts worth running
- **Map the 47 MLB SLEP supply chain.** Birdon has a $191M 10-year ceiling. Who are the subcontractors? (Cummins engines are known from press.) The FY25 prime $49M is visible, but subaward pulls via `usa_client.get_subawards()` would reveal the OEM tier.
- **Identify the six Afloat Global C4ISR Installation IDIQ winners' comparative shares.** Press named VT MILCOM as one winner; the data shows M.C. Dean + VT MILCOM + PRISM + Serco + HII Fleet Support + (one more TBD) collectively holding the $2.45B ceiling. Ratio of FY25 $ each prime captures from the IDIQ is a competitive-position indicator.
- **NSWC specialization map.** Crane (EW + towed arrays), Newport (photonics + sonar), Philadelphia (HM&E + machinery), Panama City (MCM + diving), Carderock (hydrodynamics + model basins). Each has its own J020 / N020 contracts flowing.
- **CG integration through Navy vehicles.** Vector Planning's $27M USCG C5I contract is written via Navy NIWC Atlantic SEAPORT-NXG. This pattern - CG buying installation-engineering expertise through Navy vehicles - is likely broader and worth quantifying across all four PSCs.
- **SEAPORT-NXG penetration.** $83M of the $0.98B total flows through SEAPORT-NXG IDVs across multiple primes. That's the PRO catch-all vehicle for anything marine-equipment-flavored.
