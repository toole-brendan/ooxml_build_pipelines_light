================================================================================
DDG-51 FLIGHT III — UNIFIED SYSTEM MODULE CROSSWALK
================================================================================

Two parallel procurement streams feed every DDG-51:

  STREAM 1 — GFE (Government Furnished Equipment)
    Navy buys directly from system OEMs → delivers to shipyard for installation.
    Source: P-8a budget exhibit (per-ship cost) + USAspending (contract vehicles).

  STREAM 2 — CFE (Contractor Furnished Equipment)
    Shipyard (HII/BIW) buys from its own supply chain → installs during construction.
    Source: USAspending subawards under prime construction contracts, coded to SWBS.

All FY26 $ values in thousands ($K). GFE per-ship = P-8a FY26 / 2 ships.
CFE per-ship = HII FY18-27 subaward total / ~10 hulls (approximate).

Generated: 2026-04-14

hardware directly from the OEM (often on the SAME production vehicle as newbuild,

(V)1 for new ships). Installation labor is contracted separately (GDIT, Delphinus,
etc.) at $8-13M per ship per availability. These are upgrades to IN-SERVICE ships.

NOTES AND CAVEATS
================================================================================

1. GFE $/ship is from P-8a FY26 budget (2 ships planned). These are budget
   estimates, not actuals. Actual obligations may differ.

2. CFE $/ship is averaged across ~10 HII hulls (FY18-27). Per-hull costs vary
   significantly by Flight (IIA vs III) and hull number (learning curve).

3. BIW CFE data is almost entirely missing (0-24 subawards reported vs HII's
   2,355). The CFE figures represent HII's supply chain only.

4. The CFE total ($152M/ship) covers only SWBS-coded subcontracts. HII also
   has subawards WITHOUT SWBS codes (outsourced structural work, capex items)
   totaling ~$50-80M across FY18-27, which are not included here.

5. CIWS, RAM, Standard Missile, and other weapons are multi-platform systems
   (DDG, CG, LCS, LHA, FFG, allied navies). The contract vehicles above serve
   ALL platforms, not just DDG. The P-8a per-ship figure is DDG-specific.

6. HELIOS directed energy weapon (~$100M, Lockheed Martin) is not visible in
   USAspending — likely procured via OTA through ATI consortium.

7. "Misc. Ordnance" ($89M/ship) and "Misc. Electronics" ($47M/ship) are
   aggregated P-8a categories. Breaking these further requires the classified
   P-8a detail annexes.

8. Modernization per-module costs come from OPN/WPN budget sublines. Each
   system upgrade (SPY-6 backfit, SEWIP install, Aegis baseline upgrade) has
   its own budget line AND contract vehicle, making it fully traceable.

9. Depot maintenance per-module costs are NOT publicly available. MSMO
   availabilities are single task orders covering all work. Per-system
   breakdown exists only in TYCOM repair packages, contractor bids, and
   the Navy's VAMOSC cost visibility database.

10. The DDG Mod Backfit (DDGM BF) task orders ($8-13M per ship) cover
    physical alteration installation labor — the system hardware is procured
    separately on the OPN production vehicles above.
