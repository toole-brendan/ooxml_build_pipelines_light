# Top-Down TAM: US Navy & Coast Guard In-Service Ship Work

## 1. Total TAM Headline

**FY2026 Top-Down TAM: ~$32.0 billion** (scope-adjusted, deduplicated)

| Taxonomy Category | FY24 Actual ($M) | FY25 Enacted ($M) | FY26 Request ($M) | 3-Year Avg ($M) | Share of FY26 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Maintenance — Depot/Industrial** | 13,884 | 14,323 | 17,527 | 15,245 | 54.8% |
| **Maintenance — Organizational** | 1,665 | 1,648 | 1,352 | 1,555 | 4.2% |
| **Modernization — Program** | 8,678 | 8,463 | 8,650 | 8,597 | 27.0% |
| **Services** | 3,712 | 3,711 | 3,868 | 3,764 | 12.1% |
| **End-of-Life** | 283 | 1,010 | 583 | 626 | 1.8% |
| **TOTAL** | **28,222** | **29,156** | **31,980** | **29,786** | **100%** |

The FY2026 figure is the President's Budget *discretionary request*. Several line items carry significant "mandatory/reconciliation" amounts not included in the base request (notably OPN BA1 Line 26 and multiple WPN lines). When reconciliation is included, the effective TAM is likely **$3–5B higher** than the base figures above.

## 2. Trend: FY24 → FY25 → FY26

| | FY24 → FY25 | FY25 → FY26 | FY24 → FY26 CAGR |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| **Maintenance-Depot** | +3.2% | +22.4% | +12.3% |
| **Maintenance-Organizational** | -1.0% | -18.0% | -9.9% |
| **Modernization-Program** | -2.5% | +2.2% | -0.2% |
| **Services** | 0.0% | +4.2% | +2.1% |
| **End-of-Life** | +256.5% | -42.3% | +43.5% |
| **TOTAL** | **+3.3%** | **+9.7%** | **+6.5%** |

**Key drivers of FY26 growth:**

- **OMN 1B4B Ship Depot Maintenance** jumped from $11.8B to $13.8B (+17.3%), driven by labor cost escalation, OPTEMPO demands, and MSC M&R transfer into the account.
- **SCN CVN RCOH** surged to $1.78B (full funding for CVN-75 Harry S. Truman RCOH), up from $811M in FY25.
- **USCG depot maintenance** saw a 5.7× increase ($70M → $400M fenced in appropriations language) — a major policy shift.
- **SEWIP electronic warfare** grew from $182M to $461M as SOEA Phase 1 procurement begins for smaller combatants.
- **CANES** grew from $436M to $529M as shipboard network/cyber modernization accelerates.

**Offsetting declines:**
- Maintenance-Organizational dropped because FY26 OMN 1B1B shows a lower discretionary request ($5.35B vs. $7.26B FY25), though reconciliation brings it back to ~$7.3B.
- WPN Tomahawk Mods collapsed in the base request ($297M → $6M), though $474M in reconciliation is expected.
- End-of-Life normalized from the FY25 spike ($1.01B) down to $583M, though NIMITZ inactivation planning keeps it elevated.

## 3. Public vs. Private Split

The public/private split is estimable only for the largest maintenance category. **Public naval shipyard revenue was not separately captured in the NWCF bronze extraction** — this is a known gap. The estimates below use historical NAVSEA benchmarks.

| Segment | FY26 Total ($M) | Public Yard ($M) | Private Sector ($M) | Basis |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Ship Depot Maintenance (OMN 1B4B) | 13,803 | ~7,600 (55%) | ~6,200 (45%) | Historical NAVSEA public/private ratio |
| CVN RCOH (SCN) | 1,779 | 0 | 1,779 | RCOH performed at HII Newport News |
| OPN Modernization Equipment | ~6,257 | — | ~6,257 | Defense primes (HII, GD, L3Harris, RTX, etc.) |
| WPN Weapons Modifications | ~177 | — | ~177 | Defense primes |
| **Identifiable Total** | **~22,016** | **~7,600** | **~14,413** | |

**Interpretation:** Of the ~$32B total TAM, roughly **$7.6B (24%)** flows through the four public naval shipyards (NNSY, PSNS, PHNSY, PNS) and **at least $14.4B (45%)** is identifiably private-sector. The remaining ~$10B (31%) is a mix: services/overhead, USCG work, organizational-level maintenance, and items where the public/private allocation cannot be determined from budget books alone.

**The private-sector addressable market is at minimum $14.4B and likely $18–20B** when accounting for the private-yard share of OMN 1B4B (~$6.2B), private-sector services, and USCG contracts.

## 6. Hybrid Items Summary

**$8.3B (26% of FY26 TAM) lives in the maintenance/modernization gray zone.**

Items flagged as hybrid in the gold layer:

| Item | FY26 ($M) | Why Hybrid |
|---|---:|---|
| Ship Depot Maintenance (OMN 1B4B) | 13,803 | Fleet alterations (TYCOM-funded modernization) embedded in maintenance account |
| Ship MR&M Equipment & Material (OPN Line 26) | 2,393 | Name explicitly spans "Maintenance, Repair and Modernization" |
| CVN RCOH | 1,779 | Combines refueling (maintenance) with capability upgrades (modernization) |
| Submarine Systems (batteries, etc.) | 922 | Batteries are maintenance consumables procured through OPN modernization |
| Surface Combatant Mod (HM&E) | 872 | HM&E includes both modernization and repair-grade replacements (AER-type) |
| LCS In-Service Modernization | 340 | Explicitly in-service work blending maintenance and capability upgrades |
| Radar, Sensors & IW (In-Service Radars) | 535 | Includes both sustainment (replacement arrays) and modernization (ECPs) |
| Amphibious Ship Midlife | 167 | Midlife programs combine restoration and capability upgrades |
| USCG Vessel Programs (SLEPs/MMAs) | 422 | SLEPs and MMAs blend maintenance and modernization by definition |
| LCAC Programs | 37 | Corrosion repair + electronics replacement + C4N upgrades |
| Other hybrid items | ~50 | LCC ESLP, sub batteries, OPN LCAC |

**Implication:** The Navy's own taxonomy enforces a hard maintenance/modernization boundary, but approximately one-quarter of the budget lives on or near that boundary. This matters for market sizing because different competitive dynamics, contract types, and incumbency patterns apply to maintenance vs. modernization work.

## 8. Known Gaps

### Data Not in These Budget Books

1. **NWCF public naval shipyard revenue** — The four public yards (NNSY, PSNS, PHNSY, PNS) operate under NWCF, but their revenue was not separately captured in the bronze extraction. The NWCF rows captured are dominated by Fleet Readiness Centers (aviation depot). This prevents a precise public/private split.

2. **NAVSUP/DLA parts and consumables** — NWCF SM-Navy ($9.2B total, ~$1.8B ship-related estimate) was excluded due to double-count risk, but some portion represents genuinely additive supply chain spend not captured in maintenance contract values. The VACL Material Strategy ($909M investment) specifically targets submarine maintenance.

3. **RDT&E pipeline** — Research, Development, Test & Evaluation spending that precedes procurement (e.g., technology development for future ship modernization programs) is not in these books.

4. **O&MN,R (Reserve)** — Reserve component ship maintenance, if any, is in a separate appropriation not analyzed here.

5. **Mandatory/reconciliation amounts** — Multiple line items show large reconciliation amounts not in the base request:
   - OPN BA1 Line 26: +$2.64B reconciliation on $5.38B base (total ~$8.02B)
   - WPN Tomahawk Mods: +$474M reconciliation on $6M base
   - Standard Boats: +$815M reconciliation on $52M base
   - These represent an additional **$3–5B** in effective TAM that is obscured in the base request figures.

6. **Contract services not in budget line items** — Engineering and technical services, fleet logistics, and other contracted support that may not appear as identifiable line items in these books.

7. **Allied/FMS maintenance** — Foreign Military Sales maintenance and repair work at US yards (e.g., allied nation ship repairs) is not in these Navy budget books.

8. **Intermediate-level maintenance** — OMN does not have a clean SAG for intermediate maintenance in the summary tables we extracted. This level of work (IMA/tender/RMC) may be embedded in other SAGs.

### Scope Uncertainty

Nine gold rows carry "Low" confidence ratings, representing ~$3.8B FY26 in aggregate. The weakest estimates are:
- Navy organizational-level maintenance (10% of OMN 1B1B = $535M) — could be 5% or 20%
- USCG cutter organizational O&M ($817M) — rough scope percentages on both total and depot
- Availability pre-planning ($860M) — 50% scope estimate
- SCN PY cost growth ($507M) — 30% in-scope is approximate

## 9. Comparison to Prior Work

The Phase 3 budget item catalog identified **113 line items** with an aggregate of approximately **$13.7B** in relevant budget lines. This gold-layer analysis captures **90 bronze rows** rolling up to **32 gold rows** totaling **~$32.0B FY26**.

**Why the difference:**

| Factor | Impact |
|---|---|
| OMN 1B4B now captured at full value ($13.8B) | Phase 3 may have captured this at a sub-SAG level or partial scope |
| OMN 1B5B Depot Operations Support ($2.8B) now included as Services | May not have been in Phase 3 scope |
| Availability pre-planning ($860M) and org-level maintenance ($1.4B) now included | Broader scope definition |
| CVN RCOH ($1.8B) and SCN items now captured | Separate appropriation not in Phase 3 O&MN focus |
| USCG items ($1.1B) now included | Phase 3 may have been Navy-only |
| WPN weapons modifications ($177M base) now included | Separate appropriation |
| End-of-Life ($583M) now included | May not have been in Phase 3 scope |
| Scope adjustments (partial percentages) applied | Increases some, decreases others |

The Phase 3 $13.7B figure likely represents the **core depot maintenance + closely related OPN lines** — essentially the OMN 1B4B account. This gold analysis deliberately casts a wider net per the taxonomy (organizational maintenance, services, end-of-life, USCG, SCN, WPN) while applying scope adjustments to keep only the ship-relevant portions.

**Sanity check:** OMN 1B4B alone ($13.8B FY26) is consistent with Phase 3. The additional ~$18B comes from: modernization equipment procurement (~$8.6B), services/overhead (~$3.9B), organizational maintenance (~$1.4B), RCOH (~$1.8B), end-of-life (~$0.6B), USCG (~$1.1B), and weapons systems maintenance (~$1.1B).

## 10. Recommendations for Next Steps

### When FY2027 Books Arrive (~2–3 weeks)

1. **Update bronze extraction** for OMN, OPN BA1, OPN BA2, OPN BA4, SCN, and WPN. The same grep-then-read approach from the implementation plan applies.
2. **Extract NWCF public naval shipyard revenue** — this was the biggest gap. The FY27 NWCF book should have a depot maintenance business area for the four naval shipyards separate from the FRC sections. This is critical for the public/private split.
3. **Capture reconciliation/mandatory amounts** — FY27 books may present these differently. Multiple FY26 line items have reconciliation amounts that more than double the base request.
4. **Compare FY26 enacted vs. FY26 request** — the FY27 book will show FY26 enacted figures, allowing validation of request-to-execution conversion rates.

### Bottom-Up USAspending Validation (Priority)

The top-down TAM is a *ceiling* derived from what the government budgets. The bottom-up analysis should validate:

1. **Private-sector contract flow** — Filter USAspending by NAICS 336611 + NAVSEA contracting offices to see how much actually reaches the private sector via contracts.
2. **Ship-class allocation** — Contract descriptions often identify the ship class, enabling the class-level TAM breakdown that budget books cannot provide.
3. **Contract type mix** — What share is firm-fixed-price vs. cost-plus? This affects margin structure and addressable market attractiveness.
4. **Incumbent concentration** — Which contractors capture the most? HII, GD/NASSCO, BAE Ship Repair, and others.
5. **Subcontracting tier** — How much of prime contract value flows to subcontractors? This identifies the sub-tier addressable market.

### Analytical Priorities

1. **Resolve the public/private split precisely** using NWCF shipyard data or NAVSEA industry day briefings. The current 55/45 estimate is a benchmark, not data.
2. **Separate the reconciliation/mandatory layer** — Build a "base request" TAM and a "total including reconciliation" TAM. The gap between them (~$3–5B) represents funding that is expected but not in the base.
3. **Build a forward-looking model** — Combine the top-down TAM with NVR fleet composition, CBO per-class maintenance cost estimates, and NAVSEA availability schedules to project 5-year TAM trajectory.
4. **Cross-reference with CBO/GAO maintenance backlog** — The gap between funded maintenance and required maintenance (the deferred maintenance backlog) represents unfunded TAM. GAO has estimated this at $1.5–2.0B+ for the Navy.
5. **Map TAM to BuildCo's addressable segments** — Not all $32B is addressable. Narrow to the specific work types, ship classes, and maintenance levels where BuildCo can compete.
