## Key Findings

1. **New construction is the largest single bucket.** Bucket 1 (~$46.3B, 62% of full TAM) is dominated by submarines: Columbia ($10.9B) + Virginia ($11.1B) = 47% of Bucket 1. $24.5B of FY26 SCN is OBBA/Working Families Tax Cut Act reconciliation funding.

2. **Depot maintenance is the core of in-service work.** Bucket 2 (Scheduled Depot Maintenance) represents 48% of in-service TAM at $13.6B. OMN funds labor/overhead ($10.9B); OPN funds material/equipment ($2.4B) for the same physical availabilities.

3. **Modernization is the fastest-growing in-service segment.** Bucket 4 ($6.6B, 23% of in-service TAM) is driven by DDG Mod ($1.0B), SEWIP ($461M), CANES ($530M+), Ship Missile Support ($456M), and fleet-wide combat system refresh.

4. **The MSC transfer inflates FY26.** The $1.584B MSC M&R transfer from NWCF to OMN in FY26 is an accounting change, not new work. Excluding it, underlying Bucket 2 growth is ~5%, not 10%.

5. **RCOH dominates Bucket 5 and is inherently lumpy.** CVN 74 STENNIS has been in RCOH since 2021 ($483M cost-to-complete in FY26). CVN 75 TRUMAN starts FY26 ($1.78B). When two RCOHs overlap, Bucket 5 spikes.

6. **Public yards handle ~27% of in-service work by funding flow.** The four naval shipyards ($7.8B) perform nearly all submarine depot work and a share of carrier work. Surface combatant depot maintenance flows almost entirely to private industry. New construction (Bucket 1) is 100% private industry.

7. **Bucket 7 is a floor, not an estimate.** The $228M identified for availability support captures only explicitly identifiable lines. True Bucket 7 could be $1-2B if scaffolding, crane services, shore power, etc. were separated from Bucket 2 availability contracts.

8. **Coast Guard is ~3% of in-service but growing.** USCG in-service vessel work (~$848M) includes growing SLEP/MMA programs. USCG new construction (~$1.3B) includes OPC, FRC, PSC, and WCC programs.

9. **84% of in-service TAM is high-confidence.** $23.9B comes from named budget lines with clear boundaries. Bucket 1 (new construction) is also high-confidence — sourced directly from SCN P-1 TOA figures.

## Methodology

- **Source:** FY2026 President's Budget justification books -- 9 books processed (OMN, OMN Vol2, OPN BA1/BA2/BA4/BA5-8, SCN, USCG, NWCF)
- **Taxonomy:** Seven work-type buckets derived from official Navy/CG definitions (JFMM, OPNAVINST 4700.7N, NEM), budget structure (OMN/OPN/SCN appropriation logic), and contract language (FPDS PSC codes, award descriptions). See `docs/Seven_Bucket_TAM_Crosswalk.md`.
- **Architecture:** Medallion method -- Bronze (raw extraction), Silver (canonicalization, disposition, overlap resolution), Gold (bucket classification, TAM table, reconciliation)
- **Figures:** All in then-year dollars as presented in budget books, thousands ($K). FY26 includes both President's Budget Request and OBBA/Reconciliation amounts.
- **Performer data:** OMN Vol2 OP-5A shipyard data used for public/private split context (not additive to TAM)
- **Bucket 1 metric:** Total Obligation Authority (TOA) from SCN P-1, including net procurement, SFF, AP, and CTC. USCG PC&I newbuild programs from Congressional Budget Justification.
