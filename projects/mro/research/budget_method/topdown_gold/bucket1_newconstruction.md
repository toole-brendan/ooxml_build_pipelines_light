## Key Observations

1. **Bucket 1 is the largest single bucket.** At ~$46.3B (FY26), new construction exceeds the entire in-service TAM (~$28.6B). Combined 7-bucket TAM is ~$75B.

2. **Submarines dominate.** Columbia ($10.9B) + Virginia ($11.1B) = $22B = 47% of Bucket 1.

3. **Reconciliation is a major factor.** $24.5B of $47.4B FY26 SCN (52%) is in the Reconciliation column. Programs like DDG-51, LPD, LHA, MLS, and T-AO are almost entirely Reconciliation-funded in FY26.

4. **Year-to-year volatility is extreme.** FY24 $28B → FY25 $38B → FY26 $46B. Driven by ship authorization timing, multi-year AP phasing, and the unprecedented FY26 Reconciliation amounts.

5. **100% private industry performer.** All Bucket 1 work flows to private shipyards. Public naval shipyards do not perform new construction.

6. **Top 5 programs by FY26 TOA:**
   - Columbia Class: $10.9B
   - Virginia Class: $11.1B
   - DDG-51: $5.4B
   - LHA: $3.9B
   - CVN-81: $3.3B
