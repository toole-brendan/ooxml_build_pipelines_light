## 2. Known Exclusions

1. **Classified programs** -- Nuclear reactor specifics (Reactor Components $399,603K included at top-line but detail classified), BFTN classified, Fixed Surveillance excluded. Impact: Likely minimal incremental; top-line dollars are captured.

2. **Army watercraft** -- Not in Navy/CG budget books. Separate service, separate appropriation. Would add modest TAM ($50-200M est. based on known Army vessel fleet).

3. **OPN BA3 (Aviation Support)** -- Not extracted. Carrier aviation support systems (AAG, EMALS) installed on in-service carriers are a potential edge case. Deprioritized. Impact: Small.

4. **WPN Book** -- Weapon procurement not extracted. Ship-installed weapon system sustainment may have small components not captured in OPN BA4. Impact: Small.

---

## 3. Known Data Gaps

1. **USCG O&S vessel share** -- The 60% allocation of Object Class 25.7 is an estimate. True figure requires CG-internal cost data. Impact: Could shift USCG Bucket 1/2 by +/- $100-200M.

2. **Bucket 7 undercount** -- Availability support is largely embedded in Bucket 2 contracts. True Bucket 7 could be $1-2B if scaffolding, crane services, shore power, environmental, drydock services, and force protection were separated out. This makes the current $228M figure a floor, not an estimate.

3. **MSC granularity** -- $1.584B MSC M&R is a single line transferred to OMN in FY26. No breakdown by ship class or availability type. Also creates FY24/FY25 comparability issue (MSC was in NWCF before FY26).

4. **Public-private split precision** -- The four-shipyard figure ($7.79B, ~56% of 1B4B) is from OMN Vol2 OP-5A. It includes overhead and infrastructure costs, so the true "depot maintenance execution" share may differ. Contract-level data (bottom-up) needed for precision.

5. **In-service share of OPN BA2/BA4** -- Several lines have estimated in-service percentages. True splits require program-office-level data on new-build vs. in-service equipment allocation.

6. **FY24/FY25 USCG detail** -- USCG figures assumed flat FY24 approximately equal to FY25 for ISVS sub-investments. Actual year-over-year variation within sub-investments not available from single-year justification format.

7. **Contractor Logistics Support (CLS)** -- CLS spending is distributed across buckets by predominant work type. No way to isolate total CLS from budget books.

## 4. Sensitivity Analysis

How the TAM changes under different assumptions:

| Scenario | Effect on TAM |
|----------|---------------|
| USCG vessel share of O&S 25.7 is 50% instead of 60% | In-service TAM decreases by ~$103M |
| USCG vessel share of O&S 25.7 is 70% instead of 60% | In-service TAM increases by ~$103M |
| Bucket 7 embedded costs fully separated from Bucket 2 | Bucket 7 increases by $1-2B; Bucket 2 decreases by the same amount. Net TAM unchanged (reallocation between buckets). |
| OPN BA2/BA4 in-service shares all set to 100% instead of partial | Bucket 4 increases by ~$385M |
| MSC M&R excluded (FY26 one-time transfer from NWCF) | Bucket 2 decreases by $1,584,000K |
| OBBA Reconciliation excluded from Bucket 1 | Bucket 1 decreases by ~$24.5B (from ~$46B to ~$22B) |
