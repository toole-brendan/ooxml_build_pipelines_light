## 1. The load-bearing correction: watercraft procurement is in OPA BA3&4, not BA1

The prior corpus session (and `download_budget_books.py`) assumed *"Army watercraft procurement
lives in OPA BA1 (Tactical & Support Vehicles)."* **It does not.** The BA1 book carries only the
appropriation-wide **P-1 index** line (line 108/109/110, BA=**03**, BSA 55 "Rail Float
Containerization Equipment") — no dollars. The actual funding tables + narrative live in **OPA
Volume 3 = BA3 & 4 "Other Support Equipment & Spares"**, which was never downloaded. The prior log
even flagged it as a next step ("detail at Vol 3 p.257") without realizing Vol 3 is a separate book.

OPA is published in 3 volumes (per the BA1 front matter): Vol 1 = BA1, Vol 2 = BA2
Comms/Electronics, **Vol 3 = BA3 & 4**. Watercraft sits in Vol 3.

**Fix:** added OPA BA3&4 to `download_budget_books.py` (new `OPA3` dict, tier 1), corrected the
docstring scope note, re-ran (idempotent: 45 skipped, **6 new OK, 0 miss**), and converted
(`convert_to_txt.py`: 51 total, 0 empty). Filenames drift hard year-to-year and FY2025's title
literally reads "Other Support **Vehicles**" (an Army typo, but the real URL) — URLs found via
asafm.army.mil web search, header set unchanged from the corpus session. New books: 593–638 pp each.

## 3. The exhibit grammar (so the next editor doesn't re-derive it)

Funding comes from **Exhibit P-40, "Budget Line Item Justification" → "Resource Summary"**. It is a
fixed **12-column** block parameterized only by the PB year Y (label OCO↔OOC drifts; columns are
derived from Y, never from labels):

```
Prior Years | FY(Y-2) actual | FY(Y-1) enacted | FY(Y) Base | FY(Y) OCO | FY(Y) Total
            | FY(Y+1..Y+4) outyears | To Complete | Total
```

- **Gotcha:** the **Exhibit P-5 (Cost Analysis)** on a later page has its *own* "Resource Summary"
  but only **6 columns** (stops at the budget year). The parser gates on `Exhibit P-40` so the P-5
  block is ignored cleanly. (P-5 also carries the per-modernization-effort cost breakout — see §6.)
- Cells: `-` → 0.0 (DoD "no funding" convention); `Continuing` → blank (only ever in
  To Complete / Total — never in an analytical per-FY column); numbers strip commas → float.
- Measures captured: `procurement_qty`, `gross_weapon_system_cost`, `net_procurement_p1`,
  `total_obligation_authority`.

## 4. Outputs — `workbook/extracted/` (generated by `research/budget_materials/extract_budget_cuts.py`)

- **`budget_line_items.csv`** (3 rows) — dimension table: BLI, title, program, appropriation/BA/BSA,
  + **seed** analyst tags (`mission_family`, `product_layer`, `tag_confidence=seed`, `scope_note`).
  The tags are editable judgments, not derived fact.
- **`budget_funding_facts.csv`** (864 rows) — **tidy/long**: one row per
  (line_item × source_book_fy × measure × column). `column_role` ∈ {prior_years, actual, enacted,
  request_base, request_oco, request_total, outyear, to_complete, total} + `observed_fy`, `amount`,
  `page`, `source_id`. 864 = 6 books × 3 items × 4 measures × 12 columns (exactly complete).
- **`source_log.csv`** (6 rows) — the six OPA BA3&4 books → `11_Source_Log` (id, title, fy, url,
  sha256_16, pages, pdf/txt paths, access_date).

**Why long/tidy:** "requested vs enacted" is *derived in the workbook*, never pre-differenced. The
same FY appears as `request` in its PB book, `enacted` one book later, `actual` two books later —
the long shape preserves every vintage's own view (the methodology's "keep requested and enacted
in separate columns; don't difference across books blindly" rule).
