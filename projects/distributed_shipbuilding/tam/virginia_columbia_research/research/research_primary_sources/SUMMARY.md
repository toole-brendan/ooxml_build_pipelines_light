## TL;DR — the deck's three best primary-source statements on outsourcing

1. **Navy's "Golden Fleet" 30-year plan (May 12, 2026):**
   "Currently only 10% of shipbuilding occurs at distributed sites. The Navy
   aims to increase this to 50%." — **Official Navy policy: 5x outsourcing
   expansion target.**

2. **HII Q1 2026 official guidance (May 5, 2026):**
   "We are on track to grow our outsourcing hours year-over-year by 30%."
   — Direct CEO statement; confirmed as a formal operational initiative.

3. **GAO-26-109068 (April 22, 2026 testimony):**
   "DOD does not know how much funding it expects to need — beyond the more
   than $10 billion DOD already invested — to solve submarine industrial
   base challenges."

These three together let the deck make a defensible primary-source claim:
**Outsourcing in submarine new construction is not just rising — it's policy.**

## Headline executive findings

### HII (Chris Kastner CEO + Tom Stiehle CFO) — INCREASING outsourcing

- **FY2024 Q3 (Nov 2024):** "There's going to be a greater percentage of our
  work outsourced going forward... we will outsource more, which means it
  costs more."
- **FY2024 Q4 (Feb 2025):** When Wolfe Research analyst characterized the
  outsourcing increase as 30%, Kastner: "It's a material number."
  Stiehle: tied to "20% more earn throughput."
- **FY2024 Q4 (Feb 2025):** "I'd rather develop outsource partners and have
  an arms-length relationship. I really don't want to vertically integrate."
- **FY2025 Q1 (May 2025):** Analyst now cites 35% outsourcing increase.
  Kastner: outsourcing is on schedule, quality is good, "we're going to
  continue it. We need to. The industrial base is expanding."
- **FY2025 Q3 (Q3 2025):** Outsourcing now listed as a formal revenue-growth
  driver in HII earnings highlights.
- **FY2026 Q1 (May 5, 2026):** "We are on track to grow our outsourcing
  hours year-over-year by 30%." Strategic framework called
  "distributed shipbuilding strategy."

### GD (Phebe Novakovic CEO + Danny Deep COO) — INVESTING in own yards

Different stance than HII. GD's response to supplier-base constraint is
capital expenditure, not outsourcing expansion.

- **FY2025 Q4 (Jan 2026):** "Supply chain remains the gating item...
  sole-source suppliers where they are bottlenecks." 79% CapEx increase to
  >$900M, "half at least of the CapEx for this coming year is at Electric
  Boat."
- **FY2026 Q1 (Apr 2026):** Single-source supplier issue still binding;
  Columbia hours earned up 29% YoY; sequence critical material received up
  52% YoY.

### Navy policy — OFFICIAL distributed shipbuilding target

- **May 12, 2026 (Golden Fleet 30-yr Plan):** "Increase nationwide
  distributed shipbuilding work from 10 to 50 percent." $6.2B for submarine
  industrial base.
- **May 12, 2026 (FY27 Shipbuilding Plan):** "The Navy will evaluate
  overseas options and whether allied and partner shipbuilding can
  supplement domestic production."

### BlueForge ground-truth — AML3D example

- **May 6, 2026:** AML3D (Australian additive manufacturing) received
  ~$2.6M from BlueForge for "five replacement components for US Navy
  submarines, utilising ARCEMY 3D metal printing." First concrete primary-
  source identification of a real BlueForge downstream supplier — and a
  direct archetype Saronic could compare to.

## Critical caveat — the make/buy ratio gap

**Even with executive commentary, no source gives a clean "X% of contract
value is purchased/subbed" for submarines.**

What we have instead is:
- The DIRECTION (everyone agrees: outsourcing is going up)
- The CHANGE MAGNITUDE (HII: +30% YoY in hours; Navy target: 10% → 50%)
- The STRATEGIC POSTURE (HII outsourcing; GD investing in own yards)
- The SCALE OF INVESTMENT ($10B+ from DOD; $6.2B in latest Navy plan)

The deck's funnel still uses a 50/60/65% band for "outsourced inside Basic
Construction" — that band is industry-analyst consensus, not primary-source.

But we now have STRONG independent supporting evidence that outsourcing is
material and growing, sourced directly from Navy policy + HII CEO + GD CEO.

## Deck implications

1. **Slide on "Outsourcing is policy, not just trend"** — add the Navy
   10% → 50% target as headline (it's stronger than any analyst estimate).

2. **Slide on "What HII is telling the street"** — gets significantly more
   weight. The 30% YoY outsourcing growth quote is the single strongest
   evidence we have. Add Kastner's "material number" exchange too.

3. **NEW comparison slide: HII vs GD strategic posture** — HII outsources,
   GD invests in own yards. Two structurally different responses to the
   same supplier-base constraint. Useful for showing the contradiction (and
   Saronic-relevant: which model are they going to fit into?).

4. **BlueForge downstream slide** — AML3D is a real, named, recently
   identified BlueForge sub-recipient. We can use it as the archetype for
   "what does it look like to get into the MIB on-ramp."

5. **Honest gap statement on make/buy** — keep the 50/60/65% band, but
   replace the "no primary source" language with the stronger framing:
   "Navy policy commits to 5x distributed shipbuilding expansion; HII
   formally guides to +30% YoY in outsourcing hours; the exact baseline %
   of submarine contract value that is purchased/subbed is not publicly
   stated by either prime or by Navy, so the deck uses a 50-65% band."
