*This article describes the structure and visible dollar flow of work that the U.S. nuclear submarine "primes" — primarily General Dynamics Electric Boat — outsource to other firms during construction of Virginia-class attack submarines and Columbia-class ballistic-missile submarines. For the ship classes themselves, including operational and design history, see Virginia-class submarine and Columbia-class submarine. For the related but distinct surface-combatant industrial base, see Construction of Arleigh Burke-class destroyers.*

# Outsourced work on U.S. nuclear submarine construction

**Outsourced submarine work** is the portion of the United States Navy's Shipbuilding and Conversion appropriation for Virginia-class (SSN-774) attack submarines and Columbia-class (SSBN-826) ballistic-missile submarines that flows from the prime construction contract to firms other than the prime shipyard. It includes (1) first-tier subcontracts under the prime's own contracts, (2) government-furnished equipment procured by the Navy under separate prime contracts and installed by the prime shipyard, and (3) Maritime Industrial Base supplier-development spending that is contractually funded through the Columbia prime but distributed to small and mid-tier suppliers, workforce-training partners, and capacity-expansion projects.[^repo-readme]

U.S. nuclear submarines are built under a long-standing two-yard teaming arrangement in which **General Dynamics Electric Boat** (GDEB) is the contractual prime of record on every Virginia and Columbia construction contract, and **HII Newport News Shipbuilding** (HII-NNS) participates as a team partner with a publicly described workload share of approximately 50% on Virginia construction and approximately 22% on Columbia construction.[^repo-readme] Because HII-NNS work flows *through* the GDEB prime contracts and is not separately awarded, it is largely invisible in federal prime contract records and only partially visible in subaward records routed through the EB prime.[^repo-lessons-v1]

This article does not present an audited revenue figure. The headline figures it cites are aggregates of publicly visible contract-action data drawn from the Federal Procurement Data System (FPDS) Atom Feed, USAspending.gov's `/api/v2/subawards/` endpoint, and the Navy's FY2027 President's Budget *Shipbuilding and Conversion, Navy* (SCN) Justification Book.[^repo-fpds-summary][^repo-usaspending-summary][^repo-scn-extract] They are best read as a **publicly visible flow estimate with asymmetric coverage gaps**, not as a strict mathematical floor: three structural blind spots (federal naval shipyard depot work, the HII-NNS team-build share, and classified payload modules) and several pull-design constraints exclude flow that pushes the true total upward, while the absence of USAspending v3 subaward deduplication on the largest recipients with long-running primes can push specific subaward lines downward; these are all discussed under [Limitations and blind spots](10-limitations-and-blind-spots.md).

The publicly visible combined annual outsourced flow rose from approximately **$1.4 billion in fiscal year 2018** to approximately **$6.3 billion in fiscal year 2024**, with the bulk of the FY23–FY24 increase driven by the Submarine Industrial Base supplier-development ramp on the Columbia Build I+II prime contract `N0002417C2117`, where **BlueForge Alliance** appears as a $4.21 billion FY16–FY25 cumulative consortium subaward recipient.[^repo-summary-findings][^repo-subaward-top]

### Federal acquisition policy

- **Federal Acquisition Regulation, Subaward Reporting (FAR 52.204-10).** Defines reportable first-tier subcontracts as direct contractor subcontracts for performance of a prime contract, with a reporting threshold above $30,000, and excludes certain long-term supplier agreements and indirect / general-and-administrative items. The reporting requirement implements the Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act of 2006 (FFATA, Pub. L. 109-282).[^far-52-204-10]
- **Federal Acquisition Regulation, Government Property (FAR Part 45 and FAR 52.245-1).** Defines Government-Furnished Property (GFP) as property in the possession of, or directly acquired by, the U.S. government and subsequently made available to the contractor for performance of a contract. Government-Furnished Equipment (GFE) is the equipment subset of GFP and is the term used in this article for major submarine components such as the naval reactor plant, the Trident weapon system, and combat-system elements furnished to the prime shipyard.[^far-part-45]

Returns paginated XML at 10 records per page with `<link rel="last">` indicating the last page's start offset. Query syntax: field:value pairs joined by spaces (implicit AND), quoted phrases for multi-word values, square-bracket ranges for dates and numerics. Three record types: `<ns1:award>` (standard FAR-based contract action), `<ns1:OtherTransactionAward>`, and `<ns1:OtherTransactionIDV>`. [^usaspending-api]: USAspending.gov, REST API, base URL `https://api.usaspending.gov/api/v2/`. The `POST /search/spending_by_award/` endpoint accepts a payload of `filters` (including `award_type_codes`, `award_ids`, `time_period`) and `fields`, and returns awards with their `generated_internal_id`. The `POST /subawards/` endpoint accepts an `award_id` (the `generated_internal_id`), `limit`, `page`, `sort`, and `order`, and returns first-tier subawards with six fields (`id`, `subaward_number`, `description`, `action_date`, `amount`, `recipient_name`). Award-type-code groups (Contracts ABCD, IDVs IDV_*, Grants 02–05, Other 06–11/-1) cannot be mixed in a single call; HTTP 422 is returned on a cross-group request. Practical record ceiling is approximately 2,000 per prime.

[^repo-usaspending-script]: submarine_outsourced_work, `scripts/pull_usaspending_subawards.py`. Python 3 script implementing the two-call USAspending workflow.
