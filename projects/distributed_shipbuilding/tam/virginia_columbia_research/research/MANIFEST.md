## Methodology — annual outsourcing rollup

Three measures of "outsourcing by primes" are extracted, each with different
strengths/weaknesses:

### 1. Direct first-tier subaward $ visible in USAspending (`subaward_annual_by_prime.csv`)
The most direct measure of what GDEB pays to subs. Pulled from USAspending
`/api/v2/subawards/` per prime PIID, bucketed by subaward `action_date` FY.

**Strengths:** Real $ flows. Vendor-named at the recipient level. PIID-attributed.

**Weaknesses:**
- 2,000-record cap per prime (hits ceiling for Virginia Block V/VI + Columbia Build I/II)
- FFATA reporting gap: $30K threshold + non-compliance (GDEB does report, but coverage is incomplete)
- Lags prime obligation by 6-18 months — fresh contracts (e.g., N0002424C2110 Block VI LLTM) show fewer subs than they really have
- Intra-corporate subs (e.g., GD Mission Systems as sub on Lockheed Trident) inflate raw aggregates; need to roll up parents carefully

### 2. GFE prime spend (`fpds_annual_by_prime.csv` filtered to BPMI + LM + others)
The portion of the submarine SCN dollar that goes to NON-shipyard primes directly
from the Navy (i.e., GFE that the shipyard installs but did not contract for).

**Strengths:** Captures the BPMI naval-reactor flow + LM combat systems + LM Trident
that NEVER appears as a sub of GDEB because it's a separate prime relationship.

**Weaknesses:** Some of this overlaps with what's in SCN Plans / Electronics / Ordnance
cost categories — you can't simply add this to subaward $ without double-counting against
the SCN top-line.

### 3. SCN cost-category breakdown (`scn_li_cost_categories.csv`)
The official Navy view of cost composition per ship. The cost categories themselves are
a rough proxy for "in-house vs outsourced":

| Cost Category | Likely composition |
|---|---|
| Plan Costs | Lead-yard services, MIB supplier development (this is where the $1.3B+ MIB pass-through lives in SSBN 837 Plans), engineering |
| Basic Construction/Conversion | Shipyard labor + hull material — mostly in-house at GDEB/NNS |
| Change Orders | Mixed |
| Electronics | Largely outsourced (LM combat systems, NG sonar, L3Harris photonics) |
| Propulsion Equipment | Largely GFE (BPMI reactor) |
| HM&E (Hull, Mechanical, Electrical) | Mix of in-house + outsourced (pumps, valves from Curtiss-Wright; piping; auxiliaries) |
| Ordnance | GFE (LM Trident D5/D5LE2 for Columbia) |
| Other Cost | Miscellaneous |

**Strengths:** Official Navy attribution per cost element per ship.

**Weaknesses:** Coarse-grained; no vendor-level detail; the category-to-outsourced mapping
above is interpretation, not a formal Navy taxonomy.

## Known caveats (read before drawing conclusions)

1. **HII-NNS submarine share is invisible in federal procurement data.** Per the
   teaming agreement, NNS does ~50% of Virginia construction and ~22% of Columbia. But
   in FPDS, only GDEB appears as the prime, and NNS submarine work shows up as either
   subcontracts on GDEB primes (some, but likely understated) or routed through Northrop
   Grumman (some of the NG-as-sub-of-GDEB amount). The only reliable HII-NNS sub split
   comes from HII 10-K segment disclosures + analyst estimates.

2. **Cumulative vs. window-period dollars.** FPDS `totalObligatedAmount` is the running
   total at the latest mod. For annual rollups we MUST use per-mod `obligatedAmount` by
   signed-date FY (which is what `fpds_annual_by_prime.csv` does). Don't sum the
   "cumulative" totals across mods — that triples or quadruples counts.

3. **Subaward 2,000-record cap.** USAspending `/api/v2/subawards/` caps at ~2,000 records
   per prime. For the two big GDEB masters (N0002417C2100, N0002417C2117) we hit the cap,
   meaning the long tail of small subs is missing. We sorted by amount descending so the
   top spend IS captured.

4. **MIB / BlueForge Alliance.** The largest single sub recipient on Columbia
   (N0002417C2117) is BlueForge Alliance at ~$4.2B. BlueForge is a consortium pass-through —
   it then distributes to small/mid-tier suppliers for facility build-out, workforce
   training, and equipment. So "BlueForge $4.2B" is NOT one company doing $4.2B of
   submarine work; it's the consortium itself, which has its own opaque distribution.

5. **Federal naval shipyard work is NOT outsourcing.** Norfolk / Portsmouth / Pearl
   Harbor / Puget Sound do depot maintenance and overhauls in-house with federal payroll.
   That's not what we're measuring.

6. **Classified submarine payloads are excluded.** Intelligence Community modules
   (e.g., SSGN special operations payload extensions, classified surveillance equipment)
   are invisible in FPDS by design.

7. **Date window choice (FY18 signed).** The window starts FY18 because the biggest
   still-active master contracts (Virginia Block V/VI at N0002417C2100 signed 2017;
   Columbia Build I/II at N0002417C2117 signed 2017) need to be captured with all their
   mods. Earlier contracts (e.g., Block IV N0002412C2115 signed 2012) are still active
   via residual mods and show up via their post-2018 mods.

```bash
# 4. Aggregate everything (fast, <30 seconds)
python3 scripts/aggregate_annual_outsourcing.py
```

## Open questions for the next analysis pass

These are flagged for follow-up but NOT in scope for this initial gathering:

- **Per-ship attribution.** The subaward data is per PIID (which often covers multiple
  ships in a block). Mapping the per-FY subaward $ back to specific hulls would require
  parsing mod descriptions for hull references (e.g., "SSN 812 CONSTRUCTION BOAT 2 FY 24").

- **MIB beneficiaries.** Identify who BlueForge Alliance distributes to. Their public
  reporting + DPA Title III award announcements have some of this.

- **HII-NNS submarine share.** Pull HII 10-K segment data + analyst estimates to estimate
  the NNS portion of submarine work, since it's invisible in federal data.

- **Annual outsourcing %.** Once subaward + GFE prime data is loaded, compare to the
  SCN top-line per FY to compute "% of submarine SCN $ that flows to non-prime entities".

- **Trend.** Plot annual outsourcing trend FY18 → FY26. Has the % grown with MIB
  investments? Are specific sub-domains (electronics, propulsion) growing faster than
  others?
