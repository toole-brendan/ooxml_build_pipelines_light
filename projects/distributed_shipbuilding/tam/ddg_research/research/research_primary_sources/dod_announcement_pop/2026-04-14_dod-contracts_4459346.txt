## Destroyer paragraph 1

Marinette Marine Corp., Marinette, Wisconsin, is awarded a $30,000,000 not-to-exceed undefinitized contract action for advance procurement of long lead time material and associated engineering and design activities in support of four Medium Landing Ship Block 1. Work will be performed in Marinette, Wisconsin (46%); De Pere, Wisconsin (39%); and Kenner, Louisiana (15%). Work is expected to be completed by September 2027. Fiscal 2025 shipbuilding and conversion (Navy) funding in the amount of $15,000,000 will be obligated at award and will not expire at the end of the current fiscal year. This contract was not competitively procured. This contract is based on Section 128(b) of the Servicemembers Quality of Life Improvement and National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2025, Pub. L. No. 118-159. Naval Sea Systems Command, Washington, D.C., is the contracting activity (N00024-26-C-2430).

## Destroyer paragraph 2

Raytheon, Tucson, Arizona, was awarded a $14,055,719 modification to a previously awarded contract (N00024-26-C-5434) for technical and design agent engineering in support of the Evolved SeaSparrow Missile and NATO SeaSparrow Missile Systems programs. This modification combines purchases for the Navy (66%); and the governments of Australia, Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Greece, Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Spain, and Türkiye under Memorandum of Agreements (34%). Work will be performed in Tucson, Arizona (97%); and Portsmouth, Rhode Island (3%), and is expected to be completed by Dec. 31, 2030. Fiscal 2026 research, development, test and evaluation (Navy) funds in the amount of $7,794,089 (28%); fiscal 2025 other customer funds in the amount of $6,900,597 (25%); fiscal 2026 weapons procurement (Navy) funds in the amount of $6,073,872 (22%); fiscal 2025 operations and maintenance (Navy) funds in the amount of $2,941,473 (10%); fiscal 2026 other customer funds in the amount of $2,755,875 (10%); fiscal 2026 other procurement (Navy) funds in the amount of $1,077,196 (4%); fiscal 2025 weapons procurement (Navy) funds in the amount of $382,045 (1%); and fiscal 2025 research, development, test, and evaluation (Navy) $200,000 (<1%), were obligated at time of award, of which $2,941,473 will expire at the end of the current fiscal year. Naval Sea Systems Command, Washington, D.C., is the contracting activity. (Awarded Apr. 10, 2026).
