TAKEAWAYS

  • EPS -- $4.17 per diluted share for the quarter, with net earnings of $1.143 billion.
  • Revenue -- $14.307 billion for the quarter, up 7.8% year over year, and 11.4% sequentially.
  • Operating Earnings -- $1.152 billion, up 2% year over year, and 9.1% sequentially.

  • Full-Year Outlook -- 2025 revenue up 10.1%, operating earnings up 11.7%, net earnings up 11.3%, and diluted EPS up 13.4% compared to 2024.
  • Segment Performance -- Marine Systems revenue up 16.6%, and operating earnings up 25.9%; Aerospace revenue up 16.5%, and operating earnings up 19.3% for the year.
  • Aerospace Segment -- Book-to-bill of 1.3 times in the quarter, with 158 aircraft delivered, 22 more than prior year; Gulfstream aircraft book-to-bill of 1.4 times.
  • Combat Systems -- Quarterly revenue $2.5 billion, up 5.8%, with operating margin of 15%; book-to-bill 4.3 times for the quarter, 2.1 times for the year; backlog of $27.2 billion.
  • Defense Orders -- Over $4 billion in awards for Eagle tactical vehicles in Germany; $600 million for bridges in Norway and UK; $640 million for light armored and logistics vehicles in Canada.
  • Marine Systems -- Quarterly revenue $4.8 billion, up 21.7%, with operating earnings $345 million, up 72.5%; submarine tonnage at Electric Boat up 13% year over year.
  • Technologies Segment -- Quarterly revenue $3.24 billion, flat year over year; operating earnings $290 million, down $29 million; full-year revenue $13.5 billion, and earnings $1.28 billion, with increased backlog to $16.7 billion.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $5.1 billion for 2025, free cash flow just under $4 billion; free cash flow conversion rate of 94%.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $1.2 billion for the year, up nearly 30%; $609 million in Q4 alone.
  • Debt and Liquidity -- Year-end cash balance $2.3 billion, net debt $5.7 billion, down $1.4 billion; no commercial paper outstanding at year end.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Revenue of $54.3 billion-$54.8 billion; operating margin 10.4%; operating earnings ~$5.7 billion; forecasted EPS of $16.10-$16.20; capital expenditures to rise 79% to over $900 million, equaling 3.5%-4% of sales.
  • Tariffs -- Tariff impact on Aerospace was $41 million in 2025, expected to be higher in 2026, and included in margin forecasts.
  • Submarine Programs -- Electric Boat output up, with productivity gains across all shipyards; backlog and future contract value at Marine and Combat Systems at record year-end levels.
  • Order Pipeline -- Technologies segment has a $120 billion qualified pipeline; total estimated contract value company-wide ends at $179 billion, up 24% year over year.
  • Interest Expense -- Full-year interest expense totaled $314 million; expected to increase to $340 million in 2026 if notes are refinanced at higher rates.
  • Tax Rate -- Full-year effective tax rate 17.5% for 2025, expected to remain similar in 2026.

SUMMARY

Management delivered a fiscal quarter and year marked by double-digit top-line and operating earnings growth, with backlog and book-to-bill ratios reaching historic highs across multiple business segments. Forecasts for 2026 reflected continued expansion, particularly in Marine Systems and Combat Systems, and targeted capital allocations to accelerate throughput and address strong order flows in core markets. Management emphasized operational execution, productivity advances, and measured plans to absorb sizable revenue gains while pursuing sustained margin enhancement.

  • Phebe Novakovic said, "we will be laser-focused on operations" to support delivery of the 2026 forecast.
  • The company expects increased CAPEX, mainly directed to Electric Boat, and initiatives supporting U.S. Navy production targets.
  • Free cash flow conversion is projected to return to 100% of net income for 2026, even with elevated investments.
  • Combat Systems' international growth is set to accelerate in 2027, as new European programs transition from engineering to production.
  • Management noted that tariff costs will be higher for Aerospace in 2026 than in 2025, but those impacts are built into projections.
  • Margin improvement in Aerospace is forecasted due to improved pricing, efficiencies, and lower R&D costs, with continued supply chain headwinds.
  • Technologies segment completed its transition from legacy programs, with backlog and opportunity pipeline indicating potential for durable, long-term growth.
  • The dividend remains a capital allocation priority; management reiterated its practice of not commenting on share repurchases.
  • Phebe Novakovic characterized Gulfstream's demand drivers as led by new products, and strength across key economic buyer segments.
  • Operating earnings growth in 2026 is not predicated on capital deployment, according to management guidance.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: The ratio of orders received to revenue recognized within a specific period, serving as an indicator of future growth and backlog strength in aerospace and defense contracting.
  • Free Cash Flow Conversion Rate: The proportion of net income converted into free cash flow after capital expenditures; a key metric in evaluating cash-generating efficiency.
  • IDIQ: Indefinite Delivery, Indefinite Quantity contract—U.S. government contract vehicle that allows for an unspecified quantity of goods or services during a fixed period.
  • Gulfstream: General Dynamics' business jet brand, manufacturing a range of aircraft noted throughout the call (e.g., G700, G800, G600, G650).
