RISKS

Lockheed Martin recognized $1.8 billion in total losses across legacy programs in Q2 2025, including a $950 million charge on a classified Aeronautics program, reflecting cost overruns and continuing execution risks.

The IRS asserts a $4.6 billion additional income tax liability related to an accounting method change, and is accruing $100 million in interest expense as it contests this claim.

Free cash flow in Q2 2025 was a usage of $150 million due to working capital headwinds, delayed F-35 Lot 18/19 awards, tariff impacts, and unusually high receivables at quarter-end.

2026 free cash flow is projected to decline to around $6 billion (non-GAAP), which is below the previous expectation for absolute growth, citing increased investment demands and ongoing development challenges in complex programs.

TAKEAWAYS

Sales: $18.2 billion in second quarter sales, comparable year over year and up sequentially from the first quarter, driven by growth in Missile and Fire Control, F‑35 production, and Strategic Missiles, partially offset by charges in Aeronautics and Rotary and Mission Systems.

Segment Operating Profit: $570 million segment operating profit for Q2 2025, including a $1.6 billion impact from Skunk Works and Sikorsky program charges; excluding charges, operational performance showed mid single‑digit sales growth and a high single‑digit increase in profit for Aeronautics.

Program Charges: $950 million charge for a classified Aeronautics program, $570 million loss on the Canadian Maritime Helicopter Program, and $95 million on the Turkish Utility Helicopter Program, each following customer negotiations and updated risk assessments.

GAAP EPS: $1.46 earnings per share for Q2 2025, reduced by $5.83 due to the combined effect of program losses, impairment tied to NGAD, and a tax reserve.

Free Cash Flow: Usage of $150 million in free cash flow for the second quarter, driven by approximately $600 million in F-35 working capital headwind, $100 million in tariff impacts, and delayed collections, but most expected Q2 collections were recovered in July.

Shareholder Returns: $1.3 billion returned via dividends and share repurchases in Q2 2025.

F-35 Deliveries: 50 aircraft delivered in Q2 2025, totaling 97 for the year to date, with full-year deliveries targeted at 170–190 in 2025; international demand remains high and new deals announced for the United Kingdom and Belgium.

Operating Segment Details: Aeronautics sales grew 2% year over year in Q2 2025, with an underlying mid single-digit increase excluding the classified program; Missiles and Fire Control sales rose 11%, with 6% profit growth; Rotary and Mission Systems sales fell 12% due to program losses; Space segment sales increased by 4% year over year, with profit up 5% compared to Q2 2024.

Backlog: $167 billion backlog at Q2 2025 quarter-end, with anticipated growth in the second half of 2025 from expected awards, including F-35 lots 18/19 and PAC-3 production.

2025 Guidance: Sales outlook unchanged at $73.75 billion to $74.75 billion for FY2025; segment operating profit now $6.6 billion to $6.7 billion (including charges); EPS lowered to $21.70–$22.00; free cash flow maintained at $6.6 billion to $6.8 billion.

2026 Free Cash Flow Outlook: 2026 free cash flow is expected to be closer to $6 billion, citing program investment needs and cash headwinds from development programs.

SUMMARY

Lockheed Martin's (LMT +2.00%) quarter was defined by significant program charges, driving down operating profit, GAAP earnings per share, and 2026 free cash flow projections. The Aeronautics classified program, Canadian Maritime Helicopter Program, and Turkish Utility Helicopter Program were identified as major sources of risk, resulting in material contract loss recognitions (GAAP). Management highlighted aggressive oversight reforms and customer engagement on problematic programs but acknowledged the persistence of fixed-price risks over multiple years. The company reported robust underlying demand and showcased the combat performance of flagship systems such as F-35, PAC-3, and THAAD, supporting management’s reaffirmed full-year sales guidance of $73.75 billion to $74.75 billion and backlog growth expectations for 2025.

Scott stated, "the IRS now asserts that we owe $4.6 billion of additional income tax associated with a tax accounting method change … We stand by our tax accounting method … pursuing remedies through the IRS Independent Office of Appeals and if necessary, through a judicial proceeding."

Taiclet cited the "criticality" of a classified Aeronautics program and described related charges as "disconcerting to us" underscoring the depth of the challenges but emphasized, "We will continue to ensure that every bid price proposal and contract structure does not introduce outsized or unbounded future risk."

Management expects significant backlog increases in the second half of 2025, driven by upcoming contracts across multiple programs, beyond the potential tailwinds from the Golden Dome Homeland Defense initiative, which is not yet reflected in the backlog.

Lockheed Martin described a strong production pipeline, including completion of TR-3 hardware integration on F-35s, upcoming multiyear CH-53K contracts, and successful missile tests, reinforcing the relevance of flagship systems in both U.S. and international defense planning.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

TR-3: Technical Refresh 3, a major F-35 hardware and software upgrade integrating Block 4 capabilities.

NGAD: Next Generation Air Dominance, the U.S. Air Force's program for a sixth-generation fighter aircraft.

Golden Dome: A proposed multi-layered missile defense architecture for the U.S. homeland, mentioned as a strategic initiative but not yet manifested in formal contracts.

PAC-3: Patriot Advanced Capability-3, an advanced interceptor missile for air and missile defense.

THAAD: Terminal High Altitude Area Defense, a missile defense system intended to intercept and destroy short, medium, and intermediate-range ballistic missiles.

CH-53K: The latest variant of a heavy-lift cargo helicopter developed for the U.S. Marine Corps.

LRDR: Long Range Discrimination Radar, a U.S. missile defense sensor developed by Lockheed Martin.

C2BMC: Command, Control, Battle Management, and Communications, a missile defense network supporting integrated operations.

JASSM: Joint Air-to-Surface Standoff Missile, a long-range, conventional, air-to-ground, precision-guided missile.

LARASM: Long Range Anti-Ship Missile, a precision-guided, air-launched, anti-ship missile.

HIMARS: High Mobility Artillery Rocket System, a lightweight mobile rocket launcher.

PRISM: Precision Strike Missile, an Army tactical missile system.

CPS: Conventional Prompt Strike, a U.S. Navy hypersonic weapons program.

ULA: United Launch Alliance, a joint venture between Lockheed Martin and Boeing for space launch services.

MH-60 Romeo: The U.S. Navy’s multi-mission maritime helicopter.

CMHP: Canadian Maritime Helicopter Program, a Sikorsky/Lockheed Martin program for the Royal Canadian Air Force.

TUHP: Turkish Utility Helicopter Program, a Sikorsky/Lockheed Martin military helicopter initiative for Turkey.

F-35 Lot 18/19: Contracted production batches of the F-35 fighter jet, mentioned as forthcoming backlog drivers.

ASC 606: Accounting Standards Codification Topic 606, governing Revenue from Contracts with Customers, referenced regarding IRS tax treatment dispute.
