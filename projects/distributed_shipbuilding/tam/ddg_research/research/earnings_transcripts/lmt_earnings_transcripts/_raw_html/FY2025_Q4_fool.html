TAKEAWAYS

  • Backlog -- Reached a record-high $194 billion, up $17.3 billion or 17%, equating to approximately 2.5 times annual sales and marking a fourth consecutive year of backlog growth.
  • Sales Growth -- Achieved 6% total sales growth to $75 billion, with every business segment contributing to the increase; Missiles and Fire Control sales grew 14% to $14.5 billion, while Aeronautics reached $30.3 billion, and Space grew 4% to $13 billion.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Generated $6.9 billion in free cash flow after prefunding nearly $900 million in pension obligations, exceeding prior expectations.
  • Segment Operating Profit -- Total segment operating profit rose 11% to $6.7 billion, with segment operating profit margin at 10.1% in the quarter and 10.3% for Space; Missiles and Fire Control segment operating profit margin was 13.8% for the year.
  • Earnings Per Share -- Reported EPS of $21.49 for the year, down 4% due to increased interest expense, a higher tax rate, and elevated FASCAS expense, despite higher segment profits.
  • Capital and R&D Investments -- Invested $3.5 billion in CapEx and independent research and development in 2025; planned 2026 investment is set to rise nearly 35% to approach $5 billion, a "step function increase" per management.
  • Business Segment Details -- Delivered 191 F-35 fighters (48 in Q4), 120 PAC-3 MSC interceptors, and the 750th HIMARS; Space business was awarded $1 billion+ for 18 Tranche 3 Tracking Layer satellites.
  • Production Ramp-Up and New Agreements -- Multi-year framework agreements, including one for PAC-3 MSE, will increase production from 600 to 2,000 units annually; first step in transforming defense acquisition and expanding long-term stable growth opportunities.
  • 2026 Financial Outlook -- Projected sales of $77.05-$80 billion (midpoint 5% growth), segment operating profit of $8.425-$8.675 billion (midpoint margin 10.9%), EPS of $29.35-$30.25 (midpoint is $8 above 2025 due largely to pension-related charges), and free cash flow of $6.5-$6.8 billion with $2.5-$2.8 billion allocated to capital expenditures.
  • MFC Segment Guidance -- Anticipates MFC sales growth of 14% in 2026, with margins expected to hold steady on ramping multi-year contracts yet facing possible 20-30 basis points dilution in early years.
  • F-35 Sustainment -- Management disclosed an additional $1 billion strategic investment in F-35 sustainment, aiming to address historical underfunding of spare parts and improve mission-capable rates.
  • Return of Pension Requirements -- After 2025’s prefunding, a minimum $1 billion pension funding requirement returns in 2027, which management may consider pre-funding based on cash flow performance.
  • Technological Innovation -- Demonstrated AI-enabled autonomy (autonomous Black Hawk), over-the-air Aegis updates, first X-59 flight, and “operable space-based interceptor” targeting first flight by 2028.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio -- Full-year book-to-bill stood at 1.2, underpinned by over $65 billion in new orders in the second half.

RISKS

  • Management referenced risk remaining on Aeronautics classified programs, noting "risk is gonna remain over the next few years" with proactive executive monitoring, though no new charges were reported in Q4.
  • Earnings per share declined by 4% year over year, attributed to below-the-line items, including increased interest expense, a higher tax rate, and higher operating FASCAS expense.
  • RMS (Rotary and Mission Systems) full-year segment operating profit declined 31%, primarily due to a $610 million decrease in profit booking rate adjustments driven by charges on the Canadian Maritime Helicopter Program in Q2.

SUMMARY

Lockheed Martin (LMT +2.00%) reported 6% year-over-year sales growth and a record $194 billion company backlog, supporting management’s outlook for stable, multi-year demand across all segments. New long-term agreements in missile production, including PAC-3 MSE and THAAD, are expected to triple annual capacity and reshape both investment requirements and cash flow, as described in the call. Management forecast 2026 sales growth of approximately 5%, a more than 25% rise in segment operating profit, and elevated free cash flow and capital investment, underscoring commitment to internal innovation, expanded capacity, and program execution.

  • James Taiclet described a multibillion-dollar investment to accelerate munition production over the next three years, including building facilities across five states, signaling a significant capacity expansion initiative not previously disclosed at this scale.
  • Evan Scott stated, the acceleration of production for these programs provides line of sight to a compound annual growth rate for MFC sales of at least double-digit through the end of the decade.
  • Management outlined a potential strategic shift in capital deployment, emphasizing the impact of new long-term contracts by stating, the ROIs will exceed our cost of capital, and therefore, we should invest in those stable growth opportunities over much longer terms than we've ever enjoyed before.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FASCAS: Financial Accounting Standards Codification–Accounting Standards related to pension and postretirement benefit adjustments impacting reportable earnings.
  • Block 4 Capability: Next-generation software and hardware upgrades for the F-35 program, delivering enhanced sensor, weapon, and mission-system functionality.
  • Tranche: Distinct contract phases for U.S. government satellite and defense system procurement; “Tranche 3” refers to an advanced phase in the SDA’s layered space architecture.
  • PAC-3 MSE/MSC: Patriot Advanced Capability-3 Missile Segment Enhancement/Missile Segment Configuration, representing advanced interceptor missile variants.
  • Book-to-Bill: Ratio of incoming orders to recognized revenue, measuring demand momentum and future revenue visibility.
  • Golden Dome: An envisioned integrated missile and threat detection and defense architecture for homeland security; contextually referenced as a developing U.S.-focused capability.
  • CAPEX: Capital expenditures for property, plant, equipment, and technology development (as used operationally by management).
  • Reach Forward Losses: Strategic loss accruals on certain classified or emerging-technology development contracts, occasionally required by early project estimates or risk events.
  • CH-53K: Heavy-lift helicopter program managed by Sikorsky/Lockheed Martin, referenced as a driver for Rotary and Mission Systems growth.
  • JASSM / LRASM: Joint Air-to-Surface Standoff Missile / Long Range Anti-Ship Missile — major missile programs with new production ramps impacting segment sales.
