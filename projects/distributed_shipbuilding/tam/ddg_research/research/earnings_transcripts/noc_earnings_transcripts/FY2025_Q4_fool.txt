TAKEAWAYS

Backlog -- Ended at more than $95 billion, an all-time company record, supported by over $46 billion in net awards during the year.

Book-to-Bill Ratio -- Maintained a five-year average of 1.1x; 2025 book-to-bill was about 1.17x.

Sales -- Full-year sales reached $42 billion, 3% organic increase, above prior guidance, with fourth-quarter sales of $11.7 billion, up 10% year over year and 12% sequentially.

Earnings Per Share -- Mark-to-market adjusted EPS was $7.23 in Q4, a 13% increase, and for the year, guidance set for 2026 as $27.40-$27.90, representing mid-single-digit growth.

Free Cash Flow -- $3.3 billion for 2025, a 26% increase, marking the third consecutive year of at least 25% growth.

Segment Highlights -- Aeronautics Systems Q4 sales rose 18% to $3.9 billion; Defense Systems Q4 sales up 7% GAAP, 12% organically; Mission Systems Q4 posted double-digit sales growth; Space segment Q4 sales up 5% year over year.

Operating Margin -- Segment operating margin rate was 11.2% in Q4; Space segment operating margin was 11.3%; 2026 segment operating margin is projected to remain in the low to mid-11% range.

Capital Expenditures -- Expected to increase to $1.65 billion in 2026, representing approximately 4% of total sales.

International Sales -- Achieved 20% growth in 2025, with strong demand for air and missile defense, radars, and airborne capabilities.

Major Program Developments -- B-21 achieved key milestones, including first flight of the second aircraft; multiple contract awards for LRIP Lot 3 and procurement for Lot 5; Sentinel program advancing and restructuring with the Air Force.

Production Capacity Expansion -- Tactical solid rocket motor capacity doubled since 2021 at ABL facility, with an additional 50% expansion underway and similar investments at Elkton, Maryland.

Space Development Agency Awards -- Q4 award for 18 Tranche 3 tracking layer satellites, totaling 150 satellites in SDA satellite backlog.

Pension Funding Status -- Ended the year with 11.3% asset returns and funding status of 106%, resulting in minimal required cash contributions ahead.

2026 Guidance -- Sales guided between $43.5 billion and $44 billion, with all four business segments contributing to mid-single-digit growth; projected free cash flow of $3.1-$3.5 billion.

Dividend Policy -- Company will maintain the dividend, with the next update expected in the second quarter after board review; share repurchases to pause past January.

B-21 Acceleration -- $2-$3 billion multi-year investment anticipated if agreement finalized; accelerated revenue and higher returns expected to materialize mainly from 2027 onward.

SUMMARY

Northrop Grumman (NOC +0.72%) closed the year with record backlog and full-year sales exceeding guidance, driven by robust performance across all operating segments. Management provided 2026 guidance calling for broad-based mid-single-digit revenue and earnings growth, with notable planned increases in capital expenditures to support further capacity and technology development. Capital deployment is shifting toward growth investments, limiting share buybacks and holding the dividend steady pending board review.

New CFO John D. Green was welcomed, succeeding Kenneth L. Crews following a smooth transition after a 22-year tenure.

International demand was marked by 20 countries seeking IBCS, with additional contract wins anticipated in munitions, airborne radar, and uncrewed system markets.

Supply chain capacity and investment remain focal points for scaling delivery, particularly in munitions and space, with most expansion funding directly managed through operational planning and internal investment.

2026 guidance does not yet include potential upside from an accelerated B-21 program rate or major pending contract awards, but management expects incremental growth in subsequent years as these opportunities are realized.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Book-to-Bill Ratio: The ratio of net new orders received to revenues recognized in a given period, indicating future growth momentum.

LRIP: Low-Rate Initial Production; early-stage production runs used to establish manufacturing processes before full-rate production begins.

EAC: Estimate at Completion; a forecast of the total cost of a project or program at its completion.

SDA: Space Development Agency; a U.S. government organization responsible for space system acquisitions, including missile tracking satellites.

IBCS: Integrated Battle Command System; an air and missile defense command-and-control system produced by Northrop Grumman.

GEM 63: A type of solid rocket motor developed for commercial and government launch vehicles.

Takimo: Referenced as a program ramping in Aeronautics Systems, specific details not defined in the call.

FAXX (Apex): The company's internal reference for its next-generation advanced fighter program, not yet reflected in guidance.

Sentinel: The U.S. Air Force’s next-generation intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM) program, formerly known as GBSD.

Project Talon (Talon IQ): Uncrewed collaborative combat aircraft platform, leveraging Beacon autonomy test bed ecosystem; designated YFQ 48 A by the US Air Force.

CRS: Commercial Resupply Services; NASA missions for resupplying the International Space Station using commercial contractors.
