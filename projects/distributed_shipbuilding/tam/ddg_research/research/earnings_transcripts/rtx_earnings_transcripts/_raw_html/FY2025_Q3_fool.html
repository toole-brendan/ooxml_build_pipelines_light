  • Organic Sales Growth -- Increased 13% year over year, supported by double-digit expansion in commercial OEM, aftermarket, and defense across the company.

  • Free Cash Flow -- Reported at $4 billion for the quarter, attributed to working capital improvement and advance contract payments shifted forward from Q4.

  • Full-Year Guidance Raised -- Adjusted sales outlook lifted to $86.5 billion-$87 billion (8%-9% organic growth); adjusted EPS increased to $6.10-$6.20, up from the previous $5.80-$5.95 range.
  • Commercial Aftermarket -- Up 18% in the quarter; growth driven by heavy shop visit content at Pratt & Whitney and over 15% year-to-date growth at Pratt Canada commercial aftermarket.
  • Collins Aerospace Segment -- Sales reached $7.6 billion, up 8% adjusted and 11% organically; commercial OE sales climbed 16%, aftermarket rose 13%, and defense advanced 6%.
  • Pratt & Whitney Segment -- Sales reached $8.4 billion, up 16% on both adjusted and organic bases; commercial aftermarket increased 23% and military sales rose 15%.

  • Debt Reduction and Divestitures -- Paid down $2.9 billion of debt and completed two business sales, including Simmonds Precision Products for $765 million.
  • GTF and MRO Output -- PW1100 MRO output increased 9% for the quarter, 21% year-to-date; company expects 30% MRO output growth for the full year, supported by record gate restarts and a 30% increase in repair shop completions.

RISKS

  • Neil Mitchill said, "Collins saw about $90 million of headwind from year-over-year tariffs, actually same number that Pratt saw for the quarter," indicating continued tariff-related margin pressure for both segments.
  • Full-year guidance for free cash flow is maintained at $7 billion-$7.5 billion despite higher operating profit due to "some stocking" and less inventory reduction than initially planned, which may constrain near-term cash generation.
  • Neil Mitchill stated, "On a year-over-year basis, there will be a little bit more headwind from the higher negative engine margin in the fourth quarter," particularly impacting Pratt's commercial OE results.
  • Christopher Calio stated, "It's not going to be the demand and it's not going to be the composition of the backlog. It's are we going to get the supply chain in a healthy enough place to be able to deliver at these higher rates," underscoring ongoing supply chain execution risk.

SUMMARY

Management confirmed double-digit organic growth and record backlog across all segments, with new contract wins in commercial and defense driving higher full-year sales and earnings targets. Significant operating leverage was delivered through headcount discipline and productivity initiatives, while margin enhancements resulted from favorable program mix and operational improvements at Raytheon and Collins. Strategic investments in capacity, digital tools, and innovation—such as the hybrid electric demonstrator and advanced missile systems—were detailed as critical future growth enablers.

  • Direct pricing actions in aftermarket and spare parts were described as "appropriately aggressive," driven by demand and tariffs, with continued focus on margin capture through pricing in 2026.
  • Year-to-date, over $900 million was returned to shareholders via dividends, while management prioritized continued debt reduction and maintained a stated commitment to dividend growth aligned with earnings.
  • The company reported 10 consecutive quarters of material receipts growth at Raytheon, signaling persistent supply chain focus in meeting expanding defense production schedules.
  • Collins' interiors business was singled out for top-line strength and as a margin expansion driver next year, with backlog execution and productivity cited as supporting factors.
  • Neil Mitchill highlighted a baseline free cash flow for the business of $8 billion-$8.5 billion, excluding special compensation items, suggesting improvement in cash generation capacity going into 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Book-to-bill: A ratio comparing new contract orders received to revenue billed in the same period, used as a demand signal and backlog indicator.
  • MRO: Maintenance, repair, and overhaul—refers to aftermarket engine and component servicing, a significant margin and recurring revenue stream in aerospace.
  • GTF: Geared Turbofan—Pratt & Whitney’s next-generation commercial aircraft engine line.
  • V2500: A specific aircraft engine model, widely used and forming a large installed aftermarket base for service revenue.
  • AMRAAM: Advanced Medium-Range Air-to-Air Missile, a core Raytheon program referenced for major new orders in the period.
  • GEM-T: Guidance Enhanced Missile-Tactical, a variant of the Patriot missile referenced for key international awards.
  • LTAMDS: Lower Tier Air and Missile Defense Sensor—next-generation sensor system awarded during the quarter.
  • SM-6: Standard Missile-6, a multi-mission missile referenced as part of Raytheon’s naval program volume.
  • Coyote: Raytheon’s counter-drone effector product, cited as newly awarded and advancing in capability.
  • AOG: Aircraft on ground—an operational term indicating an aircraft out of service, commonly tracked for MRO responsiveness.
  • CORE operating system: RTX’s proprietary operational improvement and productivity management process.
