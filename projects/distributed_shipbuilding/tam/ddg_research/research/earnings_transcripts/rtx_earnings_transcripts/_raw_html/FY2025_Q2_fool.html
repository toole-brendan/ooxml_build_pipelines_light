RISKS

Chief Financial Officer Neil Mitchill stated the company incurred a $72 million free cash flow outflow in the quarter, including about $250 million for powder metal compensation and $175 million from tariff impacts, and confirmed a $0.30 adjusted EPS headwind from tariffs incorporated into the full-year 2025 outlook.

Management revised downward the Collins and Pratt & Whitney segment operating profit growth guidance for 2025 versus 2024 compared to prior expectations. Collins is now expected to deliver $275 million-$350 million in adjusted operating profit growth for 2025 versus 2024 (previously $500 million-$600 million) and Pratt at $200 million-$275 million (previously $325 million-$400 million) in operating profit growth versus 2024 (full-year outlook), both due to tariffs partially offset by volume.

Calio disclosed a charge at Pratt & Whitney following an airline customer bankruptcy (on a GAAP basis), with mitigation dependent on future asset utilization and recovery.

TAKEAWAYS

Adjusted Sales-- $21.6 billion in adjusted sales, up 9% year-over-year on both an adjusted and organic basis, led by strength across all three channels.

Commercial Aftermarket Sales-- Commercial aftermarket sales rose 16% year-over-year, continuing momentum from the prior quarter and indicating strong end-customer demand.

Segment Operating Profit-- $2.7 billion in segment operating profit, representing a 12% year-over-year increase (non-GAAP) and yielding 30 basis points of consolidated segment margin expansion.

Adjusted EPS-- Adjusted earnings per share was $1.56, up 11% from the prior year, with approximately $0.06 attributed to higher tariff costs; GAAP EPS from continuing operations was $1.22, including $0.28 for acquisition accounting adjustments and $0.06 for restructuring and other items.

Free Cash Flow-- Outflow of $72 million, including about $250 million for powder metal compensation and $175 million in tariff-related costs.

Book-to-Bill Ratio-- 1.86, with total backlog at $236 billion as of the quarter, up 50% year-over-year and 9% sequentially, fueled by large commercial and defense awards.

Pratt & Whitney Orders-- Booked over 1,000 GTF engine orders, including orders for up to 177 aircraft for Wizz Air and 91 aircraft for Frontier Airlines.

Raytheon Segment Awards-- Secured over $5 billion in integrated air and missile defense contracts, with a $1.1 billion award for [AM NYMEX] effectors, noted as the largest in program history.

Isothermal Forging Output-- Increased 12% year-over-year and 10% sequentially, driving a 22% year-over-year improvement in PW1100 MRO output; management reaffirmed an over 30% MRO output growth goal for the full year to reduce AOGs.

Tariffs-- 2025 net tariff cost forecast reduced to $500 million, with about $125 million already incurred in the first half of 2025 and a full-year cash impact projected at $600 million after mitigation actions.

Full-Year Guidance Updates-- Adjusted sales outlook raised to $84.75 billion-$85.5 billion (up from $83 billion-$84 billion) for the full year, equating to 6%-7% organic sales growth for the full year; adjusted EPS (non-GAAP) range revised to $5.80-$5.95 for the full year (down from $6-$6.15); free cash flow guidance remains $7 billion-$7.5 billion for the full year.

Collins Aerospace Segment-- Sales were $7.6 billion, up 9% adjusted and organically; commercial aftermarket sales were up 13%, defense sales were up 11%, commercial OE sales were up 1% year-over-year; adjusted operating profit of $1.2 billion was up $104 million versus the prior year for Collins. Full-year sales are now expected to grow mid-single digits on an adjusted basis and high single digits organically.

Pratt & Whitney Segment-- Sales were $7.6 billion, up 9% adjusted and organically; commercial aftermarket up 19%, commercial OE sales were up 15%, military engines sales were flat. Adjusted operating profit was $608 million, up $71 million. Full-year adjusted and organic sales are expected to rise low double digits versus 2024.

Raytheon Segment-- Sales were $7 billion, up 6% adjusted and organically; adjusted operating profit was $809 million, up $100 million for Raytheon; segment backlog was $63.5 billion; Raytheon's rolling 12-month book-to-bill ratio is 1.49; full-year margin uplift attributed to favorable international program mix.

Portfolio Changes-- Collins' Siemens Precision Products business agreed for sale at $765 million, and $1.8 billion actuation business divested, with proceeds dedicated to balance-sheet strengthening.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted that a 4-week work stoppage at Pratt & Whitney contributed to a first-half free cash flow outflow but expects a full recovery in the second half. The defense backlog expanded due to North American and European demand, underpinned by major international air and missile defense wins and U.S. budget increases targetingRTX(RTX +1.00%) focus areas. RTX cited investments in autonomous and AI-enabled technology through collaborations such as Shield AI and Kongsberg as key innovation efforts supporting future growth. Recent tax legislation restoring full expensing of research and development was described as providing moderate cash benefits in 2025 and sustainable tax advantages over the long term, with the bill effective at the beginning of 2025 and ongoing benefits expected in 2026, 2027, and 2028.

President and Chief Executive Officer Christopher Calio described end-market demand as "exceptionally strong" specifically in the core defense businesses, with Raytheon backlog up approximately 25% since the end of 2023.

Calio emphasized ongoing execution and capacity investment, stating RTX is investing $250 million in defense production ramp-ups for programs like Gem-T, Coyote, and AMRAAM in 2025.

Collins reduced overdue supply line items by 25% year-to-date, evidencing tangible supply chain improvement.

Mitchill affirmed that, factoring in $1.2 billion in powder metal payments, operational free cash flow (non-GAAP) for the full year is expected to be about $8.5 billion—more than 100% of adjusted net income for the full year.

With an 8% dividend increase, RTX now projects $37 billion of capital will be returned to shareholders from the date of the merger through year-end 2025.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Book-to-Bill Ratio: Calculated as orders received divided by revenue recognized within a period, indicating demand relative to supply and backlog expansion or contraction.

AOG (Aircraft on Ground): Industry term for aircraft that are grounded due to maintenance needs, affecting operational capacity.

MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul): Set of activities and services to maintain, repair, or overhaul aircraft engines and systems for operational readiness and safety.

GTF (Geared Turbofan): Pratt & Whitney’s commercial engine platform known for higher fuel efficiency and lower emissions.

OE (Original Equipment): Initial installation of systems or components on new aircraft produced by manufacturers.
