Takeaways

Organic revenue growth -- Revenue increased by $600 million to $5.7 billion, marking 15% organic growth, with nine out of the last 10 quarters achieving organic growth.

Backlog -- The backlog nearly doubled to over $40 billion, representing two times the company's annual revenue, excluding $25 billion of pending Munitions Acceleration Council (MAC) orders under negotiation.

Book-to-bill ratios -- Total book-to-bill ratio was 1.4, while international book-to-bill reached 2.2.

Segment operating income -- Segment operating income rose $125 million to $902 million.

Segment margin -- Segment operating margin was 15.7%, up 10 basis points year over year.

GAAP EPS -- GAAP earnings per share were $2.72, up 33% year over year.

Free cash flow -- Free cash flow was an outflow of $187 million, which management characterized as typical for the first quarter due to working capital timing.

Space & Mission Systems revenue -- This segment delivered $3 billion in revenue, up 24%, benefiting from a new classified program milestone; segment margin expanded by 60 basis points.

Communication & Spectrum Dominance revenue -- Revenue was $1.9 billion, reflecting 3% growth, with margin up 60 basis points.

Missile Solutions segment -- Segment revenue was $1 billion, up 18%, with operating margin at 12.5% (an increase of 110 basis points), supported by higher production volumes and asset sales, partially offset by unfavorable EAC adjustments.

International revenue growth -- International business accelerated above 20% growth, and $460 million in international resilient communications orders were booked with three NATO countries.

Major contract awards -- The company secured a>$2.2 billion international missionized business jet program with an initial $726 million order, and two Royal Canadian Air Force contracts totaling approximately $700 million.

Munitions Acceleration Council programs -- $25 billion in orders for these programs remain under negotiation and are expected to convert to contracts by year-end.

Capital structure developments -- A $1 billion Department of War investment for Missile Solutions was received, and a confidential Form S-1 for an IPO of Missile Solutions (to be named "Axyv") was filed.

Productivity gains -- Revenue per employee increased by almost 25% over the past couple of years, attributed to productivity improvements and technology investment including AI.

R&D and innovation spend -- Innovation and capacity investments rose 44% in the quarter, with R&D spend noted at approximately 2.5%-3% of revenue, plus over $1 billion in customer-funded R&D contracts contributing to a roughly 10% all-in innovation rate.

2026 outlook -- Management reaffirmed full-year revenue guidance at $23 billion to $23.5 billion (7% organic growth at the midpoint), segment operating margin guidance in the low 16% area, and free cash flow guidance of $3 billion.

EPS guidance increase -- GAAP EPS guidance for the year was raised by $0.10 at both ends to $11.40 to $11.60.

Missile Solution IPO and portfolio actions -- The segment is included in current 2026 and 2028 guidance; impacts from the planned IPO, Department of War investment, or the planned majority sale of space propulsion are not yet reflected and will be considered upon execution.

Summary

Management emphasized a strategic alignment with increased global defense priorities, highlighting a near doubling of backlog and strong international demand. The company reported significant classified and missile segment growth, noting asset sales and a $1 billion Department of War investment as key strategic actions. R&D investments and productivity initiatives created efficiency gains, while large multiyear contract negotiations in munitions and communications are expected to further drive performance. Guidance updates maintained revenue and margin targets while increasing EPS. Portfolio shaping through an IPO of Missile Solutions and divestitures remains ongoing, with expected incremental updates upon deal closure.

The new missile company will be named "Axyv," following a confidential S-1 filing and rebranding inspired by missile guidance terminology.

International book-to-bill reached 2.2 for the quarter.

Management stated, "Segment operating margins have now increased for the tenth consecutive quarter," reflecting operational discipline and program execution across segments.

The installed base of software-defined radios surpassed 1 million units worldwide, with management planning to expand it by 20% over the next couple of years to serve evolving communication requirements.

Classified work represented 28% of total company revenue, up from the previous year. Management sees classified work continuing to grow as a share of the business mix.

Book-to-bill performance and backlog expansion were achieved without inclusion of the $25 billion in pending MAC orders, suggesting further backlog growth.

Management confirmed asset sales contributed approximately 30-40 basis points to segment margin in the missiles business this quarter, offset by negative EAC adjustments.

International ISR business pipeline is estimated at roughly $40 billion, signaling substantial long-term opportunity outside the domestic market.

Industry glossary

Book-to-bill ratio: The ratio of new orders received to revenue recognized in the same period, indicating future demand versus delivered performance.

ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance): Mission systems and platforms focused on collecting, analyzing, and disseminating battlefield intelligence.

SDA (Space Development Agency): U.S. agency tasked with developing new military space capabilities, including missile tracking satellite constellations.

HPTSS (Hypersonic and Ballistic Tracking Space Sensor): A defense program for missile tracking satellites focused on addressing hypersonic threats.

MAC (Munitions Acceleration Council): U.S. Department of War council directing and accelerating missile and munitions procurement.

EAC (Estimate at Completion): A project management metric projecting the final cost of a program or contract.

ID/IQ (Indefinite Delivery / Indefinite Quantity contract): A contract allowing multiple orders over a fixed period under general terms.

NG C2 (Next Generation Command and Control): Army modernization effort to upgrade battlefield communications and control infrastructure.

SRM (Solid Rocket Motor): A propulsion technology for missiles, representing a critical component in L3Harris Technologies' supply chain and production ramp.

Falcon V / Falcon IV radios: Proprietary L3Harris Technologies software-defined radios designed for military data and communications requirements.
