  • Capital Deployment -- $890 million of capital was returned to shareowners, predominantly through dividends.

RISKS

  • The CFO stated, "the $850 million [tariff impact] is net of mitigations," but warned, "there's a lot of variability yet to come," with most financial effects expected in the back half of the year as inventory turns, and cash flow impact estimated to be 15%-20% higher.
  • Management highlighted policy fluidity, noting, "we would be impacted if the current environment were to stay in place, because not all regulatory and operational mitigations would address our tariff exposure," and confirmed the outlook excludes potential secondary impacts such as customer demand changes.
  • Collins and Pratt are expected to bear the majority of tariff impact, with each segment absorbing approximately $400 million in lost operating profit if tariffs persist throughout the year, according to the CFO.

SUMMARY

Management emphasized that free cash flow improved by more than $900 million, supporting a continued focus on capital returns and domestic investment. The call clarified that the direct tariff cost estimate of $850 million, net of mitigations, was provided for transparency but is not included in current outlooks, with most effects anticipated later in the year. A robust defense and commercial aerospace demand environment, highlighted by an 8% backlog increase to $217 billion, is reinforced by record program awards and policy shifts such as increased European defense spending and U.S. procurement reform. New certifications and production transitions—specifically for the GTF Advantage and LTAMDS—underscore strategic progress on high-growth programs positioned for future volume. Ongoing cost transformation and margin expansion across all segments, amid supply chain stabilization and selective pricing actions, contribute to the margin and profit momentum outlined for the full year.

  • Raytheon achieved a quarterly margin of 10.7% and remains on track for targeted productivity gains and further margin expansion, with the CFO reiterating confidence in returning to the "12%-plus range."
  • Expanded co-production and sustainment agreements in Europe position RTX to capture a share of the European Union's planned $850 billion defense budget increase, with Raytheon integrated air and missile defense cited as a focus.
  • The CEO acknowledged that future tariff policies could yield outcomes beyond direct costs, including operational disruptions and changes in customer buying behavior, not captured in the direct estimates shared.
  • Fleet management and shop visit assumption stability for V2500 engines is expected even in a lower-demand environment, based on current fleet age and low retirements observed in the first quarter.
  • Operational execution—including the ramp-up of MRO output, progress on supply chain initiatives, and targeted U.S. industrial investments—supports capacity readiness for increased commercial and defense demand.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • GTF: Geared Turbofan, a next-generation jet engine designed by Pratt & Whitney featuring a gearbox that optimizes fan and turbine speeds for increased efficiency, reduced noise, and improved fuel burn.
  • LTAMDS: Lower Tier Air and Missile Defense Sensor, Raytheon's advanced radar system providing 360-degree threat detection and tracking with enhanced range compared to legacy Patriot systems.
  • OE: Original Equipment, referring to new aircraft components and systems sold for factory installation, rather than aftermarket replacement or upgrade.
  • AOG: Aircraft on Ground, a status indicating an aircraft is grounded and not operational due to required maintenance or repairs, often resulting in urgent demand for parts and MRO services.
  • Book-to-Bill: A ratio measuring new orders (bookings) received relative to product shipments (billings) over a specific period; ratios above 1.0 indicate growing backlog.
  • LLP: Life-Limited Part, a critical aircraft engine component with a regulated maximum usage limit to ensure safety and reliability.
