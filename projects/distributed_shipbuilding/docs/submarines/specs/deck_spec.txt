================================================================================
1. THESIS & HEADLINE
================================================================================
The "market" is not a normal commercial category. It is a layered federal-
procurement ecosystem (SCN budget lines, Basic Construction, GFE, advance
procurement, FFATA subawards, SIB pass-throughs, HII team-build, and a large
unseen supplier layer). The deck does two jobs at once:
  (1) make the market legible, and
  (2) defend the sizing boundary.

OPERATIVE HEADLINE (annual-first; strict, non-nuclear coefficient):

    Average annual opportunity is ~$3.3B TAM and ~$2.8B broad-component SAM
    (FY22-FY27). Built as $56.6B Basic Construction at a 35.0% supplier
    coefficient = $19.8B cumulative TAM; broad-component SAM = $16.8B cumulative.

Secondary (supporting) context: the $19.8B / $16.8B cumulative figures sit
beneath the annual headline, never above it.

This is the STRICT model. It is deliberately NOT the older broad
"50-65% outsourced band" / "FY27 midpoint pool" framing used in earlier drafts.
That framing is retired. Do not reintroduce it.

2. GLOBAL RULES / HOUSE STYLE
================================================================================
(deck_core/slide_guide.md is authoritative; this section records the deck-
specific choices and the few places this deck deviates.)

2.1 PER-SLIDE CHROME (the locked quadruple)
  Every CONTENT slide carries four chrome elements (pre-wired in render(); the
  author only sets text). Cover and section dividers are exempt from the chip and
  Sources line.
    - Breadcrumb:  bold {Section}  +  " / "  +  non-bold {Topic Label}
                   (Topic Label = a near-variant of the title Topic, not identical)
    - Title:       {Topic} | {Finding}   (see 2.2)
    - Preliminary chip: auto, top-right, reads "Preliminary" (the whole deck is
                   marked preliminary; this is locked chrome)
    - Sources line: "Sources: (1) ...; (2) ...; (3) ..."  2-3 primary sources,
                   no final period
  This spec gives Section, Topic Label, Title, and Sources for every content slide
  in section 4.

2.2 TITLES
  - Format: "Topic | Finding". Topic = Title Case noun phrase; Finding = sentence
    case, concise, carries the number.
  - HARD LIMIT: the title must fit 2 lines (Arial 20pt across the content column).
    A 3-line title collides with the body box. Keep findings short.
  - The pipe "|" is the title separator (this is the one allowed bar separator).

2.3 LABELS (HYBRID convention)
  On slides, expand work-type compounds to "and" form; keep acronym compounds and
  program/agency acronyms intact. The workbook keeps its slash forms as internal
  data keys; the slide author transforms to the display label below.

  Display-label crosswalk (workbook key  ->  slide label):
    Structural fab / pre-outfit  ->  Structural fabrication and pre-outfit
    Machining                    ->  Machining
    Castings & forgings          ->  Castings and forgings
    Piping / valves / pumps      ->  Piping, valves and pumps
    Electrical / power           ->  Electrical and power
    HVAC / ventilation           ->  HVAC and ventilation
    Coatings / insulation        ->  Coatings and insulation
    Unbucketed / ambiguous       ->  Unbucketed and ambiguous
    HM&E components              ->  HM&E components            (keep)
    AP/LLTM                      ->  AP/LLTM                    (keep acronym compound)
  Keep as-is: TAM, SAM, GFE, SIB, BPMI, EOQ, POP, FFATA, SCN, NAICS, HM&E, AP, LLTM.

2.4 SEPARATORS & TYPOGRAPHY
  - No "/", "+", "x", "->" as WORD separators in titles/labels/prose. Use "and"
    or restructure ("$M / yr" -> "$M per year"; "A / B" -> "A and B").
    (Arithmetic operators inside an explicit formula or waterfall exhibit are
    fine - they are math, not word separators.)
  - No em dashes. En dashes only for number ranges; "FY22-FY27" hyphen form is ok.
  - Keep "$XXM", "$XXB", "~", "%", and acronyms. Arial everywhere. No double spaces.

2.5 TERMINOLOGY: SIB, not MIB
  - Use SIB (Submarine Industrial Base) everywhere.
  - Exactly one glossary note (appendix A1): "SIB - Submarine Industrial Base;
    earlier source files use MIB / Maritime Industrial Base."
  - Excluded SIB entities: BlueForge Alliance, Training Modernization Group, IALR.

2.6 NO SOM
  - SOM / capture / win-probability / revenue ramp is OUT of scope. Never modeled,
    never shown. SAM is a SCENARIO MENU (opportunity ceiling). Do not use
    TAM/SAM/SOM nested circles.

2.7 VISUAL HIERARCHY & FILL RESTRAINT (deck_core conventions)
  - One black-outlined object family per slide (the hero/answer/output block,
    draft slots, high-risk caveats). Secondary panels use a light GRAY_3 border
    or no fill + a bottom rule.
  - At most ONE focal filled callout per slide (_callout, <=35 words). No-fill
    commentary rails (_commentary_rail) do not count and are preferred for
    "how to read this" text beside an exhibit.
  - Fill only semantic objects (answer/KPI, boundary/denominator, evidence chip,
    method badge, unit caveat, identity, focal warning). Default no-fill/no-border
    for labels, captions, legends, axis ticks, interpretive bullets.
  - Readout restraint: do NOT end every slide with a full-width readout bar. Use a
    dedicated readout strip only for unit/denominator/scope caveats or
    reconciliation arithmetic the title and labels cannot carry. Takeaway shapes
    are allowed - just vary the form across the deck; never the same shape on all.
  - Controlled non-rect vocabulary (tags only, never panels/cards): rounded
    _classification_tag, numbered _step_dot, _chevron transitions.

2.8 IMAGES (text-driven v1; reserve the footprint)
  - Raster images are not auto-wired by the build. Use a _draft_slot stand-in
    (PRELIM-yellow fill + 1.5pt black border, italic centered descriptor) sized to
    the intended image/logo, so the footprint survives edit iterations. Replace
    with the real asset once image rel-wiring exists. Slots are named per slide
    in section 4.

2.9 TABLES
  - Native tables do not autofit. Compute per-row heights with
    text_metrics.estimate_row_heights(rows, col_w, size_pt=...) and pass the list;
    verify with slide_probe --table-fit.
  - Table skins: "dark" header for the ONE primary table on a page; "rule" (no
    header fill) for chart-side evidence tables; "light" (GRAY_1) for dense
    matrices. Keep main-deck tables short; push long lists to appendix/workbook.

2.10 CHARTS
  - Native editable charts only, from deck_core.charts: column_chart
    (mode="stacked"/"clustered"), bar_chart (mode="ranked"; horizontal, reads
    top-to-bottom, pre-sort descending), waterfall_chart, line_chart, marimekko.
    No scatter/bubble exists -> slide 18 is a scorecard.
  - Title convention: pass title=None to the factory; render the title as a
    separate _chart_title box (Arial 12pt bold, left) above the frame; place the
    graphic_frame at title_y + 380_000, rId2 (first chart on the slide).
  - Number formats: value_axis_format / value_label_format, e.g. '"$"0.0"B"' for
    $B and '"$"#,##0"M"' for $M; percent ladder uses '0.0%'.
  - WATERFALL CAVEAT (CD_08, CD_11): waterfall value labels default OFF (the
    decrease series is stored as an unsigned magnitude). Overlay signed value
    text boxes, or pair the waterfall with a small bridge table, to show the
    step values.
  - COLOR-ROLE -> PALETTE MAP (workbook color_role -> deck_core.style token):
        series1   -> BLUE_4        (primary series: Virginia BC, Annual TAM)
        series2   -> BLUE_2        (secondary series: Columbia BC, Annual broad SAM)
        start     -> BLUE_4        (waterfall start)
        decrease  -> GRAY_3        (waterfall decrease; matches factory default)
        total     -> BLUE_5        (waterfall total; matches factory default)
        accent    -> BLUE_5        (annualized-TAM marker / headline emphasis)
        reference -> GRAY_3        (non-applied coefficients)
        applied   -> BLUE_5        (the applied/headline coefficient bar)
        bucket    -> BLUE_4        (bucket bars; Unbucketed and ambiguous = GRAY_2)
        scenario  -> BLUE_4        (scenario bars; Broad component mfg = BLUE_5 focal)
        supplier  -> BLUE_5, BLUE_4 x4, BLUE_2 ... (rank tint via data_point_colors)
        sib       -> GRAY ramp     (excluded dollars read gray; BlueForge darkest)

2.11 UNITS
  - Charts: $M internally; display $0.0B (vendor bars in $M). Coefficients/shares
    one decimal percent.
