TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $3.1 billion, an increase of 13.4% year over year, with growth across all three divisions.
  • Shipbuilding Revenue -- $2.4 billion, up 17.6% year over year, modestly ahead of the $2.3 billion internal guidance for the quarter.
  • Ingalls Shipbuilding Revenue -- $725 million, a 13.8% year-over-year increase, attributed mainly to higher volume in surface combatants.
  • Newport News Shipbuilding Revenue -- $1.7 billion, a 19.3% year-over-year increase, with higher volumes across aircraft carriers, submarines, and naval nuclear support services.
  • Mission Technologies Revenue -- $748 million, a 1.8% increase year over year, driven by gains in all domain operations and unmanned systems, partially offset by lower volumes in warfare systems.
  • Segment Operating Income -- $172 million, with a segment operating margin of 5.6%.
  • Consolidated Operating Income -- $155 million, down from $161 million in the prior-year period, with a consolidated operating margin of 5%.
  • Net Earnings -- $149 million; Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $3.79, consistent with the prior year.
  • Contract Awards -- $4 billion in new contracts booked in the quarter, including a position on the $25 billion ceiling Advanced Technology Support Program, and a new $500 million Cyber Defense contract.
  • Backlog -- Increased to $54 billion, reflecting demand for shipbuilding programs.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Negative $461 million, better than internal guidance due to stronger collections and shifted disbursements.
  • Workforce -- Over 1,600 shipbuilders hired, and nearly 200 apprentices graduated in the quarter, with apprentice schools at full enrollment.
  • Shipbuilding Throughput -- On track for approximately 15% throughput improvement for the full year, in line with operational initiatives.
  • Outsourcing Hours -- Expected to grow by 30% year over year, supporting capacity expansion.
  • Guidance Reaffirmation -- Management reaffirmed all guidance elements for 2026, and the medium-term outlook.
  • Q2 Outlook -- Shipbuilding revenue projected at $2.4 billion, shipbuilding operating margins between 5.7% and 6%, Mission Technologies revenue at approximately $750 million, and operating margin near 4%.
  • Effective Tax Rate -- 20.7% in the quarter; guidance remains at 17% for the year, pending a research and development tax credit expected later in 2026.
  • Dividend -- Paid a cash dividend of $1.38 per share, totaling $54 million; no share repurchases during the quarter.

SUMMARY

Huntington Ingalls Industries (HII +0.97%) reported broad-based revenue growth led by both shipyards, with contract awards and backlog signaling sustained customer demand. Capital investment plans, expansion of the Charleston facility, and ramped workforce onboarding are positioned to elevate throughput and production efficiency. Expectations for submarine contract awards and long-lead nuclear carrier procurement were clearly outlined as upcoming catalysts, while the company highlighted medium-term upside from battleship and frigate program developments.

  • Kastner explained the company's distributed shipbuilding strategy is "on track to grow our outsourcing hours year over year by 30%," supporting customer program delivery.
  • Wilkinson noted Newport News Shipbuilding's Charleston operations contributed nearly "0.5 million earned hours of progress" in its first year, with plans to double throughput in 2026.
  • Stiehle referenced "guidance for 2026 is predicated on achieving the shipbuilding throughput improvements that we've outlined as well as reaching agreement on the next Virginia and Columbia class submarine contracts."
  • The operational cadence for major project milestones remains weighted toward the second half of 2026, including anticipated deliveries and acceptance trials.
  • Management indicated future labor growth will accompany sales growth, although outsourcing and contractor workforce strategies will partially modulate this relationship over time.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • C5ISR: Command, Control, Communications, Computers, Combat Systems, Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance; refers to integrated technological systems used in defense operations.
  • LPD: Landing Platform Dock; a class of amphibious warfare ships supporting land assault operations.
  • LHA: Landing Helicopter Assault; an amphibious assault ship class designed to deploy marine helicopters and landing craft.
  • DDG: Guided Missile Destroyer; a type of warship equipped to launch guided missiles.
  • SSN: Nuclear-powered attack submarine.
  • CVN: Nuclear-powered aircraft carrier.
  • MUSV: Medium Unmanned Surface Vehicle; an autonomous naval vessel category.
  • RCOH: Refueling and Complex Overhaul; a major midlife refit for nuclear-powered aircraft carriers.
  • Odyssey: HII's proprietary maritime autonomy software for unmanned vessel management.
  • FFX/FFG: U.S. Navy designation for new generations of guided missile frigates.
