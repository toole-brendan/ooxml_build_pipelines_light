TAKEAWAYS

Total Revenue -- $21.9 billion, representing 5% organic growth across all segments.

Adjusted Segment Operating Margin -- 15.8%, up 40 basis points due to cost efficiencies and delivery performance.

Non-GAAP EPS -- $10.73, up 11%.

Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $2.8 billion, an increase greater than 20% with contributions from growth, working capital management, and favorable tax strategies.

Fourth-Quarter Revenue -- $5.6 billion, up 6% organically, with 15.7% segment operating margin, up 40 basis points.

Backlog -- Record level exceeding $38 billion; book-to-bill achieved was 1.3.

Awards -- $2.2 billion award from South Korea for airborne early warning jets, approximately $200 million for an international weather satellite, and over $200 million in international tactical communications and radio orders; $850 million SDA contract for 18 satellites in the tracking layer tranche three; subsequent selection for over $2 billion in multi-aircraft special mission jets (initial $700 million to be booked in 2026).

2026 Guidance -- Revenue expected between $23 billion and $23.5 billion, implying 7% organic growth at midpoint, segment operating margin in the low 16% range, and free cash flow of $3 billion.

2026 GAAP Diluted EPS Guidance -- Range of $11.30 to $11.50; a transition from non-GAAP reporting as LHX NEXT program implementation completes.

Segment Guidance -- SMS expected to deliver $11.5 billion revenue (mid-10% margin); CSD expected at $8 billion revenue (25% margin); MSL at $4.4 billion revenue (mid-12% margin, expected EBITDA of approximately $620 million).

Divestiture and IPO -- 60% sale of civil space propulsion and power business to AE Industrial Partners; planned IPO of Missile Solutions in 2026, with Department of War investing $1 billion in preferred securities, converting at a 20% discount to IPO with 3% detachable warrants, and anticipating a single-digit equity stake.

LHX NEXT -- $1 billion cost savings commitment exceeded one year ahead of plan.

Capital Expenditures -- 2026 CapEx projected at $600 million, about 2.5% of sales; a significant 35%-40% increase from 2025, targeting modernization in solid rocket motor production.

CS Segment -- 2025 revenue $5.7 billion, 25.2% margin (up 50 bps), 4% growth; Q4 revenue $1.5 billion (up 3%), Q4 margin 24.9% (up 50 bps).

IMS Segment -- 2025 revenue $6.6 billion, 8% organic growth, 12.2% margin; Q4 revenue $1.7 billion (up 11% organically), Q4 margin 11.1% (down 270 bps), margin reduction primarily from CAS divestiture and Maritime program performance.

SAS Segment -- 2025 revenue $6.9 billion, 12.3% margin; Q4 revenue $1.7 billion (up slightly), Q4 margin 13.7% (up 290 bps); Q4 growth limited by government shutdown.

Aerojet Rocketdyne -- 2025 revenue above $2.8 billion, organic growth 12%, margin 12.5%; Q4 organic growth 12% (third straight quarter of double-digit growth), Q4 margin 11.8% (up 130 bps).

SUMMARY

L3Harris Technologies (LHX +1.87%) disclosed a multi-year strategic transformation, including segment consolidation, significant portfolio realignment, and new partnerships to align with changing defense priorities. Management confirmed construction started on expanded production for solid rocket motors, leveraging a $1 billion Department of War investment that accelerates capacity well ahead of contract awards and secures a single-digit government equity stake in a planned $4 billion-plus revenue Missile Solutions IPO. Explicitly, guidance for 2026 incorporates organic growth targets, improved profitability frameworks, and continued capital allocation toward segment-specific expansions in space, communications, and missile competencies.

Christopher E. Kubasik said, "Capacity is now the most important capability," flagging the centrality of production scaling in industry competitiveness.

Kubasik identified major contract wins, stating, "We executed on our programs, stabilizing challenging space programs, clearing delinquent rocket motor deliveries dating back to the time of our acquisition, and realizing efficiencies through LHX NEXT."

Bedingfield described strategic capital usage: "this is, you know, kind of a one-time capital investment to really and truly modernize how solid rocket motors are produced at speed and at scale."

Expansion of international business was highlighted, with Kubasik noting investment in local partnerships and supply chain: "We won key awards in Europe and Asia, leveraging a global supply base, and investing in local industry to scale capacity."

Aerojet Rocketdyne integration is being leveraged for solid rocket motor growth, with significant CapEx already invested since acquisition.

Management explained commercial margins in new segment CSD are "supported by strong program execution and investments to drive continued transformation and cost structure efficiency," indicating potential for further improvement as integration matures.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Book-to-bill: The ratio of net new orders (bookings) to revenue billed within the same period, indicating future revenue visibility.

Solid Rocket Motor (SRM): A propulsion component used in missiles and launch vehicles, manufactured and scaled in dedicated, capital-intensive facilities.

Tranche: A phase or batch within a larger government satellite contract, referenced here for space tracking layer awards.

LHX NEXT: Company-specific, enterprise-wide operational efficiency and cost-containment initiative initiated by L3Harris; now embedded as continuous improvement post-2025.

Merchant supplier: A business model in which a company provides specialized components to a range of primes or customers, without acting solely as a prime contractor.

SDA: Space Development Agency, the U.S. Department of Defense entity responsible for satellite constellation procurement.

HMS: Handheld, Manpack & Small Form Fit radios, a family of software-defined battlefield radios used by the military.

Department of War (DOW): U.S. government defense customer referenced throughout the call, contextually synonymous with Department of Defense.
