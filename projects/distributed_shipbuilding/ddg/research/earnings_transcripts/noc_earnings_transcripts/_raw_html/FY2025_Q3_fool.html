Takeaways

Revenue -- $10.4 billion, representing 4% reported and 5% organic year-over-year growth, driven by all segments except Space, where revenue declined mid-single digits year-over-year.

Book-to-bill ratio -- 1.17, indicating robust demand and backlog growth.

International sales -- Up 32% for the quarter and 20% year-to-date, reflecting increased global demand. International book-to-bill started the year at 1.45 and trended slightly below one in the second and third quarters due to timing. (fiscal year basis)

Free cash flow -- $1.3 billion free cash flow, a 72% increase year-over-year, with full-year 2025 guidance reaffirmed at $3.05 billion to $3.35 billion.

Earnings per share -- $7.67 diluted EPS, up 10% year-over-year; full-year 2025 guidance raised by $0.65 to a range of $25.65 to $26.05.

Segment operating margin -- Increased to 12.3%, 80 basis points above last year, with 11% year-over-year growth in segment operating income.

Defense Systems (DS) sales -- $2.1 billion, up 14% reported and 19% organically year-over-year, with margins rising to 11.4% and operating margin rate guidance raised to the high 10% range.

Mission Systems (MS) operating margin -- Rose to 16.7% in the segment, with 32% operating income growth and double-digit sales growth led by restricted microelectronics programs.

Space Systems sales -- $2.7 billion, a sequential increase, but down mid-single digits year-over-year due to wind-downs in two large programs.

Aeronautics Systems (AS) sales -- $3.1 billion, up 6% year-over-year; top-line guidance lowered to the high $12 billion range for 2025 due to timing delays.

Investments in capacity & R&D -- Over 4% of sales allocated to capital expenditures in the past two years and more than $2.1 billion invested in IRAD in that period.

B-21 program milestones -- Second B-21 aircraft entered flight test and transitioned to weapons integration; the company expects to receive LRIP Lot three and Lot five Advanced Procurement Awards later in 2025.

Guidance for 2026 -- Targeting mid-single-digit organic sales growth, a low to mid-11% segment OM rate, and $3.1 billion to $3.5 billion in free cash flow for fiscal 2026, excluding potential wins in FAXX or B-21 ramp acceleration.

IBCS program -- Reported 32 of 32 successful flight tests and projected as a significant double-digit growth contributor, with expected new international orders beginning in 2026.

Kuiper and ULA programs -- Gen63 XL rocket boosters powered a ULA Vulcan rocket that delivered the third batch of Amazon Kuiper satellites to orbit, with additional launches in backlog positioning Kuiper as an emerging growth driver.

Summary

Northrop Grumman (NOC +0.72%) reported diversified growth with sustained operational improvements amid adjustments to revenue guidance due to award timing. Management emphasized strategic achievements in digital transformation, capacity expansion, and program execution, including significant milestones in the B-21, GMD, IBCS, and solid rocket motor investments. Guidance for fiscal 2026 points to balanced growth across all segments, supported by global defense demand, with notable opportunity if the B-21 and FAXX programs accelerate, as discussed in the Q3 2025 earnings call. The earnings call underscored flexible investment in R&D and manufacturing infrastructure, continuous improvement in margin performance, and ongoing adaptation to policy and supply chain challenges within the defense sector.

Kathy Warden indicated, "Over the past two years, we've allocated over 4% of sales toward capital expenditures, well above industry averages," supporting future production ramp-ups.

Kathy Warden emphasized, "we are in active discussions with the customer that would enable that acceleration of production rate," though financial impacts depend on ongoing negotiations.

The company highlighted its role as "second supplier for the SM-6 missile," according to Kathy Warden, expanding its tactical solid rocket motor market presence.

Kathy Warden confirmed, "we do not anticipate any significant impact on our financial results" from the government shutdown, unless it extends past mid-November.

Segment-specific operating margin improvements occurred without the repetition of large EA adjustments from B-21, Sentinel, and microelectronics in fiscal 2026 guidance.

The Mission Systems segment expanded external customer sales through opened microelectronics foundries, reflecting diversification beyond internal programs.

Kathy Warden noted, "Our two foundries produce some of the world's most advanced microelectronics."

Industry glossary

IBCS: Integrated Battle Command System, a Northrop Grumman-developed command-and-control solution enabling the integration of disparate sensors and effectors for advanced air and missile defense operations.

LRIP: Low-Rate Initial Production, the phase in a major defense acquisition program where initial units are produced to support operational testing and early fielding.

EMD: Engineering and Manufacturing Development, the phase focused on full system development, design, and testing prior to production in government contracts.

IRAD: Independent Research and Development expenditures funded by the company to drive innovation and future program competitiveness.

SM-6: Standard Missile-6, a multi-mission missile used by the U.S. Navy for air and missile defense applications.

FAXX: The U.S. Navy’s next-generation fighter development program, referenced as a potential future revenue stream.

GMD: Ground-based Midcourse Defense, a U.S. missile defense program providing protection against long-range ballistic missiles.

Kuiper: Amazon's satellite broadband project, with launches supported by Northrop Grumman's solid rocket motors.

Takimo/Takamo: Referenced new program win for Aeronautics, likely related to the TACAMO (Take Charge and Move Out) program for nuclear command and control aircraft (as mentioned in transcript).
