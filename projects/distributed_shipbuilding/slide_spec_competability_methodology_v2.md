# Slide Spec — Competability Methodology v2 (flow-diagram treatment)

```text
SLIDE ## - COMPETABILITY METHODOLOGY - FLOW
Type: Body

Slide's purpose:
  Shows the competability methodology as a single left-to-right flow:
  the full outsourced pool is cleaned of non-addressable dollars,
  decomposed into work-type lanes where every dollar lands in exactly
  one lane, filtered through four structural tests, and emerges as a
  short named target list. The audience should grasp the whole method
  from the picture alone in under thirty seconds; text exists only to
  label the picture.

TITLE
-----
Copy:
  Competability Methodology | The outsourced pool is cleaned, split into
  non-overlapping work-type lanes, and filtered through four structural
  tests down to a named vendor target list.

HIGH-LEVEL LAYOUT
-----------------
One full-width exhibit: a five-stage left-to-right flow diagram occupying
nearly the entire body. A single thin caption strip runs beneath the
diagram. No RHS commentary column, no table. The slide is the diagram.

COMMENTARY COPY, IF NEEDED
--------------------------
Commentary 1
  Copy:
    Every dollar appears once: the lanes are mutually exclusive and
    together cover the whole addressable pool, so nothing is counted
    twice and nothing is dropped silently.
  Text / shape treatment:
    One italic line in the caption strip under the diagram, left-aligned
    under the lane stage. No box, no fill.

Commentary 2
  Copy:
    Structure over narrative: subaward filings carry no usable work
    descriptions, so the tests read market structure — who shares the
    work, who is entering, how often the buy recurs — not award text.
  Text / shape treatment:
    One italic line in the caption strip, left-aligned under the test
    stage. No box, no fill.

OTHER VISIBLE COPY, IF NEEDED
-----------------------------
Copy:
  None beyond the diagram labels and caption strip. No callout boxes,
  no working-flag chips, no stage-rail strip.

Text / visual treatment:
  Not applicable.

CHART, IF NEEDED
----------------
Not a data chart: the main exhibit is a built-shape flow diagram.

Copy / data / labels / chips:
  Stage 1 — single tall box, far left:
    "Outsourced BC pool FY22–25" with "~$24.5B" as the dominant figure
    and a two-line sub-label "Submarines ~$18.1B / DDG-51 ~$6.4B".
  Exclusion chute — branches downward off the connector between stages
  1 and 2, exiting the flow:
    "Not addressable" with two short items: "Supplier-development
    pass-through ~$4.2B" and "Navy-directed GFE $##B".
  Stage 2 — single box:
    "Addressable pool" with "$##B" placeholder figure.
  Stage 3 — fan-out into a vertical stack of eight lane bars:
    the seven work types — Structural, Machining, Castings & forgings,
    Piping, Electrical, HVAC, Coatings — each with its "$##B" figure,
    plus a gray eighth lane "Unclassified $##B — registry cleanup".
    Lane bar heights proportional to dollars once measured.
  Stage 4 — four vertical gate bars the lanes pass through, in order:
    "Concentration", "New entrants", "Cadence & churn",
    "Barrier & seeding". Each gate carries a one-word-scale sub-label:
    "shared?", "entering?", "recurring?", "enterable?".
  Stage 5 — single output box, far right:
    "Target list" with sub-label "ranked vendors per surviving lane;
    named awards".

Purpose:
  Proves the method is exhaustive and non-overlapping at a glance: one
  pool in, every dollar assigned once, explicit exits for what is
  excluded, explicit tests for what survives, one ranked list out.

Structure:
  Left-to-right funnel of five stages joined by horizontal connectors;
  total width divided roughly 15 / 12 / 28 / 28 / 17 percent across
  stages 1–5. The exclusion chute is the only downward branch and must
  visibly leave the flow. In stage 4 the lanes continue as thin
  horizontal strips passing through the gate bars; two or three lanes
  render full-strength to the output while the others fade after a gate,
  illustrating filtering without claiming results.

Text / visual treatment:
  Stage boxes in the deck's class-accent blues; the exclusion chute and
  unclassified lane in gray; gate bars darker with white labels. Dollar
  figures bold, larger than their labels. "$##B" placeholders carry the
  deck's yellow open-item highlight. All node labels six words or fewer.
  Faded lanes at reduced opacity, no outline.

Fit behavior:
  Drop the gate sub-labels first, then the stage-1 two-line sub-label.
  Never drop the exclusion chute, the unclassified lane, or the "$##B"
  placeholders — the visible gaps are the point. Do not add a sixth
  stage or any legend.

TABLE, IF NEEDED
----------------
None. No table on this slide.

OBJECT NOTES, IF NEEDED
-----------------------
  - Reading order: title, stage 1 through stage 5 left to right,
    exclusion chute, caption strip.
  - All five stages share a common vertical center line; the exclusion
    chute hangs below it.
  - Connectors are straight horizontal arrows between stages; the chute
    uses one elbow connector angling down.
  - Lane bars in stage 3 and their strip continuations in stage 4 must
    align row-for-row.
  - Intentional whitespace above and below the diagram; do not fill it
    with commentary.

SOURCES
-------
Copy:
  Sources: FSRS / SAM.gov first-tier subaward reporting, FY22–25 full
  history, Virginia / Columbia / DDG-51 prime contract PIIDs; FPDS-NG;
  SAM entity registrations; PB27 P-10 Strategic Outsourcing narrative;
  submarine industrial-base supplier-development announcements.
```
